### Key Industry Benchmarks

- **Average HOA monthly dues (Florida):** $200-$350/month per unit
- **HOA annual maintenance budget:** ~$800-$2,000/unit/year (common areas, landscaping, repairs)
- **PM maintenance spend:** ~$1,200-$2,400/unit/year ($100-200/month)
- **Average vacancy cost:** $1,500-$3,000/month per vacant unit (lost rent + turnover costs)
- **Construction punch list delays:** Cost GCs $500-$2,000/day in project holding costs
- **Government compliance admin:** 15-25% of contract value typically spent on compliance/reporting
- **Marketplace platform fees (industry standard):** 10-20% on vendor/service provider side; 5-10% on buyer side

## 2A. HOAs / HOA Management Companies

### Discovery Questions (First Meeting)

1. "How many communities do you manage, and how many total units?"
2. "Walk me through what happens when a resident reports a maintenance issue — who handles it, how long does it take?"
3. "How do you currently find and vet vendors? Do you verify insurance on every job?"
4. "What's your annual maintenance budget per community?"
5. "How much board time gets spent on vendor issues at monthly meetings?"
6. "Have you ever had a liability incident with an uninsured vendor on your property?"
7. "How do you handle emergency maintenance requests — nights, weekends, holidays?"
8. "What software do you use today? What do you love about it? What frustrates you?"
9. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how you manage maintenance, what would it be?"
10. "How do you currently track vendor performance? Do you know which vendors complete jobs fastest?"

### Pain Points (Be Specific)

1. **Vendor chaos:** Board members and managers are calling around, getting quotes, playing phone tag. There's no system.
2. **Insurance liability:** Vendors working on property without current insurance. One slip-and-fall = $500K+ lawsuit against the HOA.
3. **Board burnout:** Volunteer board members spending 10+ hours/month on vendor management instead of community governance.
4. **No competitive pricing:** Using the same 2-3 vendors for everything. No marketplace dynamics. Overpaying 15-30%.
5. **Emergency gaps:** No reliable after-hours vendor network. Burst pipe at 2 AM = frantic Google searching.
6. **Transparency gaps:** Residents don't know the status of their requests. Leads to angry emails and board complaints.
7. **Compliance tracking:** Manually collecting insurance certificates, W-9s, licenses. Stuff expires and nobody notices.
8. **Turnover between managers:** When a property manager leaves, all vendor relationships and knowledge walk out the door.

### Value Proposition (With Dollar Amounts)

*"UpTend gives your community instant access to 12 vetted home services — every vendor is insured, licensed, and background-checked before they ever set foot on your property. Our AI assistant George handles resident requests 24/7, our marketplace ensures competitive pricing on every job, and your compliance vault automatically tracks every insurance certificate, license, and lien waiver. The average community saves $300-$500 per unit per year in reduced vendor costs and eliminated liability risk."*

## 4E. Government Contract Pricing Models

### Cost-Plus (Best for uncertain scope)
- **When to use:** Disaster response, time-and-materials work, uncertain scope
- **Structure:** Vendor cost + UpTend management fee (15-20%) + platform fee
- **Pros:** Low risk for UpTend, government understands actual costs
- **Cons:** Requires detailed cost tracking, subject to audit
- **Example:** Hurricane debris removal — vendor cost $50/hour + 18% UpTend fee = $59/hour billed

### Fixed Price (Best for defined scope)
- **When to use:** Recurring maintenance, landscaping contracts, janitorial
- **Structure:** Monthly fixed fee based on defined scope of work
- **Pros:** Predictable for both parties, simpler administration
- **Cons:** Risk if scope creeps, need tight SOW
- **Example:** County building maintenance — 10 buildings, defined services, $8,500/month fixed

### Time & Materials (T&M) (Best for variable work)
- **When to use:** Handyman services, repair work, projects with variable scope
- **Structure:** Hourly rate by trade + materials at cost + markup
- **Pros:** Fair for variable work, government only pays for what's used
- **Cons:** Requires detailed time tracking, NTE (not-to-exceed) caps typical
- **Example:** Facility handyman services — $65/hour (includes platform fee) + materials at cost + 10%

### Pricing Strategy by Contract Type

| Contract Type | Recommended Model | UpTend Margin Target |

| Recurring facilities maintenance | Fixed Price | 20-25% gross margin |
| Emergency/disaster response | Cost-Plus | 15-20% management fee |

| Grounds/landscaping | Fixed Price | 20-25% gross margin |
