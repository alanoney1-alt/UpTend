## Phase 1: GBP Optimization (Day 1 of Partner Onboarding)

### 1.1 Categories
- Primary category = their exact trade (e.g., "HVAC contractor" not "Property maintenance")
- Add EVERY relevant secondary category (Google has 4,000+)
- Be specific: "Electrical panel installation" > "Electrician"

### 1.2 Services
- List EVERY service they offer with descriptions
- Match service names to pages on their website (or UpTend partner page)
- Include pricing ranges where possible

### 1.3 Business Description
- Use main keywords naturally
- Mention: what they do, where they do it, what makes them different
- Include service area neighborhoods by name
- Mention UpTend partnership for trust signal

### 1.4 Contact & Hours
- Verify phone number (use their main line, not UpTend's)
- Verify hours (especially if they offer 24/7 emergency)
- Add booking link → their UpTend partner page (uptendapp.com/partners/[slug])
- Add website link

### 1.5 Photos (CRITICAL)
- Upload completed job photos, before/afters, team on site, trucks, equipment
- REAL photos, no stock images
- Minimum: 10 photos at setup, then 1+ per week ongoing
- **Pro tip:** Photos taken on-site are geotagged → helps Google associate business with service areas
- Businesses with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more clicks

### 1.6 Q&A Section (Seed It)
Add 10-15 real questions customers ask:
- "How much does [specific service] cost?"
- "Do you offer same-day emergency service?"
- "What areas do you serve?"
- "Are you licensed and insured?"
- "Do you offer financing?"
- "How long does [specific service] take?"
- "Do you provide free estimates?"
- "What brands do you work with?"
- "Are your technicians background-checked?"
- "What's your warranty policy?"

Answer each thoroughly. This feeds Google's AI with structured answers it surfaces in search results.

### 1.7 Google Posts (Weekly)
Set up recurring content:
- Before/after photos from jobs
- Quick tips ("3 signs your AC is about to break down this summer")
- Seasonal reminders ("Spring is here. Time for irrigation checks.")
- Offers and promotions
- Every post needs a clear CTA: Call now. Book online. Get a free estimate.

## Phase 2: Reviews Engineering (Week 1-4)

### 2.1 Review Pipeline
- Create direct Google review link for partner
- Text link to customer immediately after job completion
- Ask specifically: "Would you mind mentioning the [specific service] we did for you?"
- Make it dead simple — one-tap link

### 2.2 Review Responses
Cap'n responds to EVERY review. In responses, naturally mention:
- The specific service performed
- The neighborhood/location
- Example: "Thanks for trusting us with your AC repair in Lake Nona! Glad we could get it fixed same day."
- This gives Google keyword-rich content tied to the profile

### 2.3 Review Goals
- 10 reviews in first month (minimum viable for LLM visibility)
- 25 reviews by month 3
- Target: specific services mentioned in review text (not just star ratings)
- Google AI reads review WORDS, not just stars

## Phase 3: Schema Markup (Week 1)

Add to partner page on UpTend and suggest for their own website:

### Required Schema Types
- **LocalBusiness**: name, address, phone, hours, service area
- **Service**: each individual service, linked to business entity
- **FAQ**: questions and answers (mirrors GBP Q&A)
- **Review/AggregateRating**: star rating and review count
- **GeoCoordinates**: exact business location

## Phase 5: Content for AI Visibility (Ongoing)

### Answer-Engine Pages (on UpTend)
For each partner, create content Google's AI can surface:
- "How much does [service] cost in [area]?" with real pricing
- "Best [service] in [neighborhood]" with partner featured
- Hyper-local: target specific neighborhoods and zip codes, not broad city

### Content AI Cannot Replicate
- Real job stories: what was the problem, what was found, how was it fixed, what did it cost
- This is original content AI can't generate — it's from actual experience
- Use question-based H2 headings (what gets pulled into AI Overviews)

### YouTube Shorts (Future)
- 2-3 min videos of common repairs and tips
- YouTube is 2nd largest search engine
- Google pulls YouTube into AI Overviews
