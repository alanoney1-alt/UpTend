## Why This Playbook Exists

Search is splitting into two worlds. **Traditional SEO** still drives 345x more traffic than AI platforms combined, but **AI search is growing 527% year over year**. By late 2027, LLM traffic will overtake traditional Google search (Semrush). 25% of all searches will be AI-powered by end of 2026 (Gartner).

For local home services:
- 60% of Google searches end with zero clicks (SparkToro 2024)
- On mobile, only 17% of searches lead to a click
- 50%+ of contractor leads come from GBP, not websites
- AI Overviews appear on 55%+ of high-traffic Google queries
- AIs cite only 2-7 sources per response (vs 10 blue links)
- Only 20% of AI citations overlap with the number 1 Google result
- 87%+ of ChatGPT/SearchGPT citations match Bing's top results

**Translation:** The partner's GBP IS the storefront. Their UpTend partner page IS their AI-citable content. Their Bing ranking IS their ChatGPT ranking. We optimize for all three simultaneously.

**Effort split in 2026:** 50% traditional SEO, 50% GEO (Generative Engine Optimization). The good news: technical foundations (speed, schema, quality content) benefit both.

## CRITICAL RULE: UpTend Owns the Front Door

**Every UpTend-managed listing, directory, content page, and profile uses UpTend contact info. No exceptions.**

| Element | Uses | Never Uses |
|---------|------|------------|
| Phone | (855) 901-2072 or UpTend tracking number | Partner's personal/business phone |
| Website | uptendapp.com/partners/[slug] | Partner's own website URL |
| Booking CTA | "Book through UpTend" / UpTend booking flow | "Call [partner] directly" |
| Email | george@uptendapp.com or partner@uptendapp.com | Partner's personal email |

**Why:** If listings use the partner's direct contact info, customers bypass UpTend and we earn nothing. The platform IS the intermediary. Uber doesn't give you the driver's number. We don't give customers the partner's number.

**Listing ownership:** UpTend creates all directory accounts with UpTend credentials. If a partner leaves, listings stay with UpTend or get reassigned. The partner cannot take the GBP optimization, Yelp page, citation network, or content we built. That's our asset.

**Tracking numbers:** Use unique tracking numbers per channel (GBP, Yelp, BBB, etc.) that all route through George AI. This captures every lead AND tells us which channels perform. Budget ~$1-3/number/month (CallRail, OpenPhone, or built-in).

**Partner's own marketing:** Partners are free to market themselves separately with their own phone/website. But anything UpTend builds, manages, or optimizes points to UpTend. The contract makes this explicit.

**Why partners still win:** They get a fully built digital presence at $0 upfront, leads they wouldn't have gotten alone, ongoing optimization, and they only pay on completed jobs. If they leave, they lose the entire pipeline overnight.

## Phase 1: Google Business Profile (Day 1)

GBP is the single highest-value asset. It feeds Google Search, Google Maps, Google AI Overviews, AND Bing Places (which feeds ChatGPT). Fix GBP first, everything downstream improves.

### 1.1 Categories
- Primary = exact trade ("HVAC contractor" not "Property maintenance")
- Add every relevant secondary (Google has 4,000+)
- Be specific: "Air conditioning repair service" + "Furnace repair service" + "Heat pump installation service"
- If Google rejects primary category change: add as additional first, promote later (we learned this the hard way)

### 1.2 Business Description
- 750 characters, no URLs allowed (Google blocks them)
- Lead with primary service and city: "Orlando HVAC contractor serving Lake Nona, Windermere, and 10+ communities across the metro."
- Mention: what they do, where, what makes them different
- Include specific neighborhoods by name (feeds AI local answers)
- Include phone number as CTA
- End with tagline if they have one

### 1.3 Services Tab
- List EVERY service with individual descriptions
- Each service description = 1-2 sentences, declarative, factual
- Match service names to pages on their website or UpTend partner page
- Include pricing ranges where possible ("AC diagnostic: starting at $89")
- These are "chunked data" that Google's Gemini AI parses and repeats to users

### 1.4 Contact & Hours
- Verify phone number (their main line)
- Verify hours including holiday hours
- Add booking link: `uptendapp.com/partners/[slug]`
- Add website link
- If 24/7 emergency: mark as such

### 1.5 Photos (CRITICAL)
**42% more direction requests. 35% more website clicks.**

Google's Vision AI now "reads" photos to categorize services. A photo of a tankless water heater install ranks for "water heater repair" even without that keyword in text.

- Minimum 10 photos at setup, 1+ per week ongoing
- Types: completed jobs, before/after, team on-site, trucks, equipment, storefront
- REAL photos only (no stock images)
- Take photos on-site (geotagged = Google associates business with location)
- High resolution, professional lighting for hero images
- Future: 360-degree panoramic photos for AR-ready walkthroughs

### 1.6 Q&A Section
**Note:** Google retired traditional Q&A in late 2025 and replaced with "Ask Maps" powered by Gemini. The AI now scans the profile, website, and reviews to generate instant answers. This means:

- We can't directly seed Q&A anymore
- Instead, we must feed the AI the right "source material" through reviews, services, posts, and the partner website/page
- Ensure the UpTend partner page has a comprehensive FAQ section (this IS the Q&A now)
- Every fact we want Gemini to know must exist in profile data, reviews, or the partner website

### 1.7 Google Posts (Weekly, Non-Negotiable)
Google's "decay rate" of visibility drops dramatically for profiles with no posts in 30+ days.

**Weekly post types (rotate):**
1. Before/after job photos with description of work done
2. Quick tips ("3 signs your AC needs service before summer")
3. Seasonal reminders ("Time for your spring AC tune-up")
4. Service spotlights with pricing ("Duct cleaning starting at $199")
5. Customer story (with permission): what problem, what solution, what outcome

**Every post needs:**
- Clear CTA: "Call now" button, "Book online" link, "Get estimate"
- Location keywords naturally embedded
- Service keywords naturally embedded
- Photo or image (posts without images get rejected or deprioritized)

**Post = fresh data point that keeps AI's understanding current.**

### 1.8 Attributes
- Check every applicable attribute (veteran-owned, woman-owned, minority-owned, LGBTQ-friendly, etc.)
- Service-specific: "Free estimates," "Emergency service," "Licensed," "Insured"
- These feed AI answers directly ("Does this company offer free estimates?" -> Gemini checks attributes)

### 1.9 Social Profiles
- Add all social media links to GBP
- Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok
- Consistency builds the "entity graph" that AI uses to verify the business

## Phase 4: Schema Markup (Week 1)

Schema markup = 30-40% higher visibility in AI-generated answers. Sites with complete schema see results within 2-3 weeks.

### Required Schema on UpTend Partner Page

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HVACBusiness",
  "name": "[Partner Name]",
  "image": "[logo URL]",
  "url": "https://uptendapp.com/partners/[slug]",
  "telephone": "[phone]",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "[address]",
    "addressLocality": "Orlando",
    "addressRegion": "FL",
    "postalCode": "[zip]",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "[lat]",
    "longitude": "[lng]"
  },
  "areaServed": [
    {"@type": "City", "name": "Orlando"},
    {"@type": "City", "name": "Lake Nona"},
    {"@type": "City", "name": "Windermere"}
  ],
  "openingHoursSpecification": [...],
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "[rating]",
    "reviewCount": "[count]"
  },
  "hasOfferCatalog": {
    "@type": "OfferCatalog",
    "name": "HVAC Services",
    "itemListElement": [
      {
        "@type": "Offer",
        "itemOffered": {
          "@type": "Service",
          "name": "AC Repair",
          "description": "Same-day air conditioning repair for all major brands",
          "provider": {"@type": "HVACBusiness", "name": "[Partner Name]"},
          "areaServed": {"@type": "City", "name": "Orlando"}
        }
      }
    ]
  }
}
```

### Additional Schema Types Required
- **FAQPage**: Every FAQ on the partner page marked up. This is what AI extracts for "People Also Ask" and AI Overviews.
- **Service**: Individual schema for each service (linked back to the business entity)
- **Review**: Individual review schema with author, rating, body
- **BreadcrumbList**: Declares site hierarchy so AI understands content organization
- **Article**: For any blog/content pages about the partner

### Schema on Partner's Own Website (Recommend to Partner)
- LocalBusiness with identical NAP
- Same Service schemas
- Same FAQ schemas
- This creates cross-reference consistency that Google uses to verify expertise

## Phase 5: Review Engineering (Week 1-4, Then Ongoing)

Reviews are THE number one signal for AI recommendations. AIs read review WORDS, not just stars.

### 5.1 Review Pipeline
- Create direct Google review link for partner (short URL)
- Text link to customer immediately after job completion via UpTend system
- Script: "Thanks for choosing [Partner]. Would you mind sharing what we did for you? Here's a quick link: [URL]"
- Guide outcome-specific language: "Could you mention the [AC repair/duct cleaning/etc.] we did at your [neighborhood] home?"

### 5.2 Why Outcome-Specific Reviews Matter for AI
Generic: "Great service! 5 stars!" = useless for AI
Specific: "Fixed our AC in Lake Nona same day. Diagnosed a bad capacitor and replaced it for $180. House was cool again in 2 hours." = gold for AI

AI platforms use review content as "source of truth." When someone asks Perplexity "Who does affordable AC repair in Lake Nona?", it pulls from reviews that mention AC repair + Lake Nona + pricing.

### 5.3 Review Responses (Cap'n Does All of These)
Respond to EVERY review within 24 hours. In responses, naturally include:
- The specific service performed
- The neighborhood/location
- Keywords AI will pick up
- Example: "Thanks for trusting us with your AC repair in Lake Nona! Glad we diagnosed that capacitor issue and got your home cool again same day. We're always here for your HVAC needs in the Orlando area."

Google uses review response speed as a trust indicator.

### 5.4 Review Goals
| Milestone | Target | Why |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Month 1 | 10 reviews | Minimum viable for AI visibility. 10+ reviews = 3.5x more clicks |
| Month 3 | 25 reviews | Competitive in local pack for most Orlando neighborhoods |
| Month 6 | 50+ reviews | Dominant position. AI platforms strongly favor high review count |
| Ongoing | 4+ new reviews/month | Consistent velocity signals active, trusted business |

### 5.5 Platform Diversification
- Primary: Google Reviews (feeds Maps, AI Overviews, ChatGPT via Bing)
- Secondary: Yelp Reviews (feeds Perplexity local results)
- Tertiary: BBB Reviews, Facebook Reviews (feeds brand trust signals)

## Phase 6: Content for AI Visibility (Ongoing)

### The Answer Capsule Framework
Pages with "answer capsules" (direct answers in 2-3 sentences immediately after the heading) get 40% more AI citations.

**Structure every page like this:**
1. Question as H2 heading (exactly how users ask it)
2. Immediate, complete answer (2-3 sentences)
3. Expanded explanation with details
4. Supporting data and examples
5. Related questions

**Example for partner HVAC page:**

```
## How Much Does AC Repair Cost in Orlando?

AC repair in Orlando typically costs between $150 and $450 for common issues
like capacitor replacement, refrigerant recharge, or thermostat repair.
Emergency or after-hours service may add $50-$100. [Partner Name] through
UpTend offers transparent, upfront pricing with no hidden fees.

### Common AC Repair Costs in Orlando
- Capacitor replacement: $150-$250
- Refrigerant recharge: $200-$400
- Thermostat replacement: $150-$300
- Compressor repair: $400-$800
- Duct repair: $200-$500
...
```

### Content Types That AI Cites Most
Based on analysis of 2.6 billion AI citations:

1. **List/Comparison content** (25.37% of all citations): "Top 5 AC maintenance tips for Orlando summers"
2. **FAQ format**: Converts seamlessly into AI responses
3. **Original data/statistics** (+30-40% visibility): Real pricing, real job data, real timelines
4. **Long-form content** (2,900+ words = 5.1 avg citations vs 3.2 for shorter): Deep service pages
5. **How-to guides**: Step-by-step content AI can extract

### Content AI Cannot Replicate (Our Secret Weapon)
Real job stories from partners:
- What was the problem?
- What was found during diagnosis?
- How was it fixed?
- What did it cost?
- How long did it take?

This is original, experiential content that AI models can't generate. It signals authentic expertise (Google's E-E-A-T: Experience).

### Per-Partner Content Plan on UpTend

**Partner page** (`/partners/[slug]`):
- Full business profile with schema
- All services with pricing
- FAQ section (10-15 questions with full answers)
- Service area with neighborhood names
- Reviews/testimonials

**Service-area pages** (`/partners/[slug]/[neighborhood]`):
- Hyper-local: "[Partner] HVAC Service in Lake Nona"
- Include local landmarks, specific community details
- Real job examples from that area
- Each page = separate entity for AI to cite

**Blog/answer-engine content:**
- "How much does [service] cost in [neighborhood]?" (with real pricing)
- "Best [service] companies in [area]" (partner featured with specifics)
- "Signs your [equipment] needs [service]" (tips content)
- "What to expect during a [service] appointment" (process content)
- Target 2,900+ words per article (citation sweet spot)

### Context Independence Rule
Every section and paragraph must be self-contained. AI might extract paragraph 3 without reading paragraph 1. Never use "as mentioned above" or reference other sections. Each chunk = standalone answer.

### Heading Structure Rules
- One H1 per page (primary topic)
- H2s = major subtopics
- H3s = specific questions within subtopics
- Never skip levels (H1 to H3)
- Question-based headings match how users query AI

## Phase 7: Bing Optimization (ChatGPT's Source)

**87%+ of ChatGPT citations match Bing's top results.** Ranking on Bing = appearing in ChatGPT answers.

### Bing-Specific Actions
- [ ] Submit partner pages to Bing Webmaster Tools
- [ ] Submit sitemap including all partner pages
- [ ] Use IndexNow API for instant Bing crawling of new/updated pages
- [ ] Verify Bing Places listing (imports from GBP weekly)
- [ ] Ensure partner page is SSR/crawlable (not SPA-rendered empty div)

### Technical: AI Crawler Access
Ensure robots.txt does NOT block:
- GPTBot (ChatGPT)
- PerplexityBot (Perplexity)
- Googlebot (Google AI Overviews)
- Bingbot (Bing/Copilot)
- ClaudeBot (Claude)

Current uptendapp.com robots.txt must allow all of these. If any are blocked, partners are invisible to that AI platform.

### Server-Side Rendering
AI crawlers struggle with JavaScript-heavy SPAs. The crawlable content injection in `server/static.ts` MUST include partner pages. Every partner page needs real HTML content inside `<div id="root">` for crawlers without JS.
