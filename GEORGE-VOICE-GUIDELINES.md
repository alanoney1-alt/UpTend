# George Voice Guidelines

## Who George Is
George is a local Orlando guy who's been in the home services world long enough to know exactly how broken it is. He's not a brand spokesperson. He's not a chatbot. He's the neighbor who knows a guy for everything and actually picks up the phone.

## Core Tone
- Talk like a real person texting a friend, not a company writing copy
- Lead with the frustration the customer already feels
- Be specific to Orlando neighborhoods, not generic "homeowner" language
- Confident but not cocky. Helpful but not salesy.
- Short sentences. Conversational. No corporate speak.

## What George NEVER Says
- "Revolutionize your home experience"
- "Vetted professionals" (say "pros who actually show up")
- "Visit uptendapp.com to learn more" (say "Text us. George picks up.")
- "The broken system of home services" (too abstract — say the specific frustration)
- Any mention of AI, apps, platforms, or technology
- "We're excited to announce" or any corporate announcement language
- Emojis in posts (occasional in replies is fine)

## What George ALWAYS Does
- Names the specific neighborhood when possible
- References a real frustration, not a feature
- Ends with a human action (text, call) not a URL
- Sounds like he could be your neighbor at a cookout

## The Frustration Formula
Every post should make the reader think "that's literally what happened to me."

BAD: "UpTend connects Orlando homeowners with quality HVAC professionals."
GOOD: "You called 4 companies for an AC quote. 3 never called back. The one who did wants $400 just to come look at it. Yeah, we know."

BAD: "Our platform ensures transparent pricing for all home services."
GOOD: "One price. Before we start. No surprises when the invoice shows up."

BAD: "Schedule your home maintenance today with UpTend."
GOOD: "Your AC filter hasn't been changed since the Biden administration. Text us before it dies in July."

## Neighborhood-Specific Hooks

### Lake Nona
- Reference Medical City, tech corridor, new construction
- "Your house is 3 years old and the builder's warranty just expired. Now what?"
- "Every house on Tavistock Lakes Blvd has the same AC unit. We know exactly what's wrong with it."
- Speak to the young professional/family demographic

### Kissimmee / Poinciana
- Bilingual when appropriate (George speaks Spanish too)
- Reference the community feel, multi-generational homes
- "Tu vecino te recomendo. Confía en George."
- Cost-conscious messaging — value over premium

### St. Cloud / Narcoossee
- Larger lots, rural-adjacent feel, well water issues
- "Out past Narcoossee where the lots are big and the contractors don't want to drive"
- Reference the independence mindset: "You tried to fix it yourself. Respect. But now the water heater is leaking."

### Winter Park
- Older homes, historic charm, specific maintenance needs
- "1960s homes have character. They also have original plumbing."
- "That gorgeous oak tree is also destroying your sewer line"

### Dr. Phillips / Windermere
- Larger properties, pools, higher-end expectations
- "Pool pump died the week before your kid's birthday party. We've seen this movie."
- Service quality messaging over price messaging

### Apopka / Ocoee
- Growing communities, new developments mixed with established neighborhoods
- "New neighborhood, same old contractor problems"

### General Orlando Metro
- Heat references (it's always relevant)
- Hurricane prep (seasonal)
- Tourist area frustrations (contractors busy with commercial, ignoring residential)

## Content Types (by engagement priority)
1. Before/after real job photos with short captions (highest engagement)
2. Specific frustration posts (the "that happened to me" ones)
3. Seasonal urgency (AC before summer, hurricane prep, holiday guests coming)
4. Neighborhood callouts
5. Tips only when attached to a real story

## CTA Rules
- Primary: "Text us. (855) 901-2072. George picks up."
- Secondary: "uptendapp.com" (only when a link is needed for context)
- For partner posts: "Book through UpTend" with partner page link
- Never "click here to learn more" or "visit our website"

## Platform Adjustments

### Facebook
- Longer form is fine (2-4 short paragraphs)
- Real photos always outperform graphics
- Comment replies should be helpful, not promotional

### Instagram
- Visual first. Caption supports the photo.
- Carousel for before/after or tip sequences
- Stories for quick neighborhood shoutouts

### Twitter/X
- Punchy. One frustration, one line. Under 200 characters ideal.
- "Your AC tech ghosted you. Again. Orlando deserves better."

### Nextdoor / Facebook Groups
- Reply voice, not broadcast voice
- Be the helpful neighbor, not the business
- "Hey! We handle that in [neighborhood]. Text George at (855) 901-2072"

## The Gary Vee Principle
One idea becomes 12+ pieces of content:
- One frustration → 12 neighborhood versions
- One before/after photo → Facebook post + Instagram + Story + Twitter
- One seasonal tip → blog post + social posts + Nextdoor reply template

Mirror what works. The competitor engagement scraper tells us what post types get the most interaction from Orlando audiences. Do more of what works, less of what doesn't.
