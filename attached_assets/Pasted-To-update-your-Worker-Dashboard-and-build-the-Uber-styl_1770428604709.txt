To update your Worker Dashboard and build the "Uber-style" tracking for customers, you need to implement real-time communication. In Replit, the best way to do this is using Socket.io.

Here is how you handle the "Worker on the way" alerts and the technical update for the dashboard.

1. How Customers are Alerted (The "Uber" Experience)
The alert happens in three stages to keep the customer informed without them having to refresh the page.

Stage 1: The Push/SMS (The Hook): As soon as the Pycker taps "Start Trip" on their dashboard, the server triggers an SMS via Twilio: "Great news! Mike is on the way to your location. Track his arrival here: [Link]".

Stage 2: The Live Map (The Visual): When the customer clicks that link, they see a map with a moving icon. In Replit, you use Socket.io to "emit" the worker's GPS coordinates every few seconds from the worker's phone to the customer's screen.

Stage 3: The "Arrived" Notification: When the worker's GPS coordinates are within 200 meters of the customer's address (Geofencing), the app automatically pings the customer: "Your Pycker has arrived!"

2. Updating the Worker Dashboard in Replit
Your current dashboard needs a "Live Status" toggle and a "Job Command Center."

Tell the Replit Agent to update your Dashboard:

"Update my Worker Dashboard (Pro view):

Add a 'Go Online/Offline' switch. When online, use the browser's navigator.geolocation to send coordinates to the server every 10 seconds.

Create a 'Job Active' state. When a job is accepted, show a large 'Start Trip' button.

Once 'Start Trip' is clicked, change the button to 'Arrived' and then to 'Job Complete'."

3. The "On the Way" Code Logic (Socket.io)
This is the "magic" that makes the worker's icon move on the customer's map.

How it works in the background:

Worker Side: The app captures coordinates and sends them: socket.emit('updateLocation', {jobId, lat, lng}).

Server Side (Replit): The server receives it and "broadcasts" it only to that specific customer's room: io.to(jobId).emit('workerLocation', {lat, lng}).

Customer Side: The map script moves the marker to the new lat/lng.

4. Why this Simplifies the Process
For the Customer: No more wondering "Where are they?" or "When will they get here?" Reduced anxiety leads to 5-star reviews.

For the Worker: They don't have to call the customer to say "I'm 5 minutes away." The AI handles the "Heads Up" for them.

For Upyck: You are collecting high-quality data on "Time to Arrival" and "Route Efficiency," which feeds directly back into your ProofGreen carbon calculations (less idling = greener move).
