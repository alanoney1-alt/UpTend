This is a delicate balance. You want the conversion rate of a "Fixed Price" but the protection of an "Estimate."

The secret is to frame the Field Analysis not as a way to charge more, but as a way to ensure Accuracy & Fairness. You are protecting the worker from surprise stairs, and the customer from overpaying for empty space.

Here is the 3-Step "Trust Script" to implement in the app's UX and copy.

1. The "Base Price" Guarantee (At Booking)
Don't call it an "Estimate." Call it a "Visual Quote."

The Copy: "Based on your photos, your Visual Quote is $145. This price is Guaranteed for the items visible in the picture."

The "What If" Link: Add a small link: "What could change this price?"

Popup: "Our workers are happy to do extra work! Common add-ons include: Stairs ($15), Disassembly ($20), or carrying items over 50ft."

Why it works: It tells them: "If you didn't lie in the photo, this price is real." It puts the responsibility on them to be honest, not on you to guess.

2. The "Pre-Arrival" Nudge (SMS/Push)
When the worker is 5 minutes away, send a notification that sets the stage for the audit.

The Notification:

"üöó Your PYCKER is arriving! They will do a quick 2-minute Site Verification to confirm the items and access (stairs/gate). Once you approve the final scope, the work begins!"

Why it works: It normalizes the "Audit." It‚Äôs not a "surprise inspection"; it‚Äôs a standard part of the process, like a pilot checking the plane before takeoff.

3. The "Digital Handshake" (The Moment of Truth)
This is the most critical part. Do not let the worker just say "It's gonna be $50 more." That feels like a scam. Instead, make the App be the "Bad Guy."

The Workflow:

Worker adds "2 Flights of Stairs" in their app.

Customer's Phone Buzzes: "Scope Update Request."

Customer Sees:

Base Quote: $145.00

Adjustment: +$30.00 (2x Stairs)

Reason: "3rd Floor Walk-up"

New Total: $175.00

Action: A big green button: "Approve & Start" (or "Decline Job").

Why it works: It feels professional. It‚Äôs an invoice, not a haggle. The customer isn't arguing with the guy in front of them; they are interacting with the "System."
