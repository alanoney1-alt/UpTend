This is the most critical operational guardrail. If you treat these "Consultants" like employees (training them, giving them uniforms, setting their schedule), the IRS will reclassify them as W-2, and you will owe millions in back taxes.

To keep them as 1099 Independent Contractors, you must structure the relationship around "Results," not "Control."

Here is the "Safe Harbor" Protocol for hiring and dispatching Home Consultants.

1. The Role: "Field Data Collector" (Not "Inspector")
Do not hire "Home Inspectors." That implies a licensed, regulated employee role. Hire "Field Data Collectors" or "estimators."

The Deal: "We pay you $25 to go to this address, capture a video scan, and fill out a checklist. How you get there and what tools you use are up to you."

The Deliverable: You are buying a Data Packet (The Video + The List). You are not buying their time.

2. The "3-Point" Legal Shield
To pass the IRS test, build these rules into the app:

Rule 1: No Equipment Provided (Financial Control).

They must use their own smartphone.

They must use their own vehicle.

Why: If you give them an iPad and a company car, they are employees.

Rule 2: The "Right to Reject" (Behavioral Control).

They must be able to see a consultation request and decline it without being fired.

App Logic: Show it as an "Opportunity," not an "Assignment."

Rule 3: Paid on "Output," Not "Hour" (Relationship).

Never pay hourly for this.

Pay Structure: Flat Fee ($25 per completed Report).

Why: If they take 2 hours to do a 30-minute job, that's their problem. If they do it in 15 minutes, they win. This is the definition of a contractor.
