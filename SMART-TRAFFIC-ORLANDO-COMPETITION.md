## 1. Orange County Traffic Engineering

### Who Manages Traffic Signals
**Orange County Traffic Engineering Division** — housed under the **Public Works Department** at **4200 South John Young Parkway, Orlando, FL 32839**.

- **Key Contact:** Chief Engineer **Hazem El-Assar** (as of most recent public info)
- **Project Manager:** **Hector Bertran** (TMC operations)
- The division plans, installs, constructs, and maintains all traffic control devices in **unincorporated Orange County**
- They fabricate and install over **9,000 traffic signs annually**

### Traffic Management Center (TMC)
The Orange County TMC is co-located at 4200 S. John Young Pkwy — **the same building as the FDOT District 5 Regional TMC**. Key stats:

- **~600 traffic signals** in unincorporated Orange County (likely grown to 650+ by 2026)
- **225 miles of fiber optic** connecting signals
- **110+ live traffic monitoring cameras** (non-recording, real-time only)
- **~90% of signals accessible remotely**
- Staffed by **6 dedicated TMC employees**, operating 12 hrs/day, 5 days/week
- Extended hours for major events (Orange County Convention Center shows, etc.)
- **Funded predominantly by federal grants** with small county contributions

### Systems & Vendors Currently in Use
- **Signal Controllers:** Mix of legacy controllers — Orange County has historically used **Econolite** and **Trafficware** controllers (standard across most Florida jurisdictions)
- **ATMS Software:** The County uses centralized traffic management software to remotely access and adjust signal timing. Specific vendor not publicly named but likely **Econolite Centracs** or equivalent, given Florida market dominance
- **Communications:** 225 miles of fiber backbone, supplemented by cellular for remote locations
- **Detection:** Traditional loop detectors and video detection at many intersections

### Adaptive Signal Projects
- **Sand Lake Road & Orange Blossom Trail:** An Adaptive Signal System was installed at this intersection as a pilot, showing **14% reduction in travel time** and **37% reduction in average delay** — impressive results that should have led to expansion
- A **$3.3 million federal grant** was awarded to upgrade TMC equipment and expand adaptive signal capability
- Despite these promising results, **widespread adaptive signal deployment across Orange County remains limited** — most signals still operate on fixed time-of-day plans with manual retiming

### Recent Procurements
- **Y25-1010: Traffic Signal Preventative Maintenance and Inspection Services** — Currently advertised, with sealed offers due **April 24, 2025**. Estimated cost: **$8,943,565.25**. This is a major maintenance contract — significant dollar value.
- Orange County procurement portal: https://procurement.opengov.com/portal/orangecountyfl
- Vendor registration: https://secure.procurenow.com/signup

**⚡ INSIGHT FOR ALAN:** Orange County has 600+ signals, limited adaptive deployment, a small TMC staff of only 6, and relies heavily on federal grants. They've proven adaptive signals work (Sand Lake/OBT pilot) but haven't scaled. This is a **massive opportunity** for a technology partner that can help them scale cost-effectively. The $8.9M maintenance contract shows they spend real money on signal infrastructure.

## 2. City of Orlando Traffic

### Separate from Orange County — YES
The **City of Orlando Transportation Department** maintains its own traffic signals on city-maintained roads, separate from Orange County's system. This means there are **two distinct jurisdictions** within the Orlando metro area managing signals independently.

- City of Orlando Transportation Department: https://www.orlando.gov/Our-Government/Departments-Offices/Transportation
- The City maintains signals within incorporated Orlando city limits

### Vendors & Standards
Per the **City of Orlando Signalization General Notes (updated November 15, 2024)**:
- **Emergency Vehicle Preemption:** Uses **Global Traffic Technologies (GTT, formerly 3M)** Opticom system — antenna mounted on cabinet top
- **Signal Equipment:** Follows FDOT standards for controller specifications
- The City has been actively piloting new AI-based traffic technology (see below)

### Smart City Initiatives — ACTIVE AND GROWING
The City of Orlando is **ahead of Orange County** in smart traffic adoption:

#### NoTraffic Deployment (LIVE)
- **Location:** Division Avenue and Church Street in downtown Orlando (right by Kia Center)
- **What:** 4 AI-powered sensors installed at the intersection for real-time detection and signal optimization
- **Cost:** Approximately **$30,000 per intersection** for the installation
- **Capability:** Detects pedestrians, bikes, cars, buses — uses AI to optimize both vehicle and pedestrian traffic
- **Key Quote from NoTraffic Chief Business Officer Norman Tutnauer:** "It's super accurate, we can detect pedestrians, bikes, cars, buses, whatever's out there"
- This is a **pilot/demonstration** — more deployments are expected

#### GridMatrix Deployment (LIVE in Seminole County, funded via MetroPlan Orlando)
- **15 intersections in Seminole County** with AI-powered video analytics for safety analysis
- Funded by **MetroPlan Orlando** grant
- Deployed in **October 2025** as a 7-month study
- Partner: **HDR** (engineering firm) + GridMatrix (AI analytics)
- Focus: Near-miss analysis, conflict detection, safety improvements
- Contact at MetroPlan Orlando: **Lara Bouck**, Manager of Project Development

### LYMMO Bus Rapid Transit
- Free BRT system in Downtown Orlando using **dedicated bus lanes and priority signals**
- Signal priority system is operational for LYMMO vehicles
- Uses TSP technology to extend green or advance signal changes for buses

### SunRail Signal Priority
- Part of the **GreenWay/ATTAIN Central Florida** federal program
- Adaptive signal interface with SunRail's Positive Train Control system
- Designed to coordinate traffic signals near SunRail crossings

**⚡ INSIGHT FOR ALAN:** The City of Orlando is actively piloting AI traffic tech with NoTraffic (Israeli company) right now. The $30K/intersection price point is a useful benchmark. The city is open to innovation and has already demonstrated willingness to work with startups. This is both a competitor foothold AND a signal that the market is receptive.

## 3. FDOT District 5 (Central Florida)

### Overview
FDOT District 5 covers **Brevard, Flagler, Lake, Marion, Orange, Osceola, Seminole, Sumter, and Volusia** counties — the entire Central Florida region. The district is one of the most active in Florida for TSM&O deployments.

- **Regional TMC (RTMC):** Co-located at **4200 S. John Young Parkway, Orlando** (same building as Orange County TMC)
- **Website:** https://www.cflsmartroads.com

### ATTAIN Central Florida Program (Formerly CEOC/ATCMTD)
This is the **biggest smart traffic program in Central Florida**. Key details:

- **$11.9 million FHWA ATCMTD grant** — the **largest single grant** awarded under this federal program at the time
- Combined with local investments, it's a **$65 million total program**
- **Partners:** FDOT (lead), MetroPlan Orlando, UCF, 28 local governments
- **Duration:** ~5 years from 2017 award

#### Four Sub-Programs:
1. **PedSafe** — Pedestrian/bicycle collision avoidance using CV technologies and LiDAR
   - LiDAR sensing at 2 intersections on SR 434 (Alafaya Trail) near UCF
   - C-V2X roadside units at **33 signalized intersections** in Orange County
   - Passive pedestrian detection technology deployed
   - Hardware installation **complete** as of 2021

2. **GreenWay** — Active management of **1,000+ traffic signals** regionally
   - Advance sensor technology
   - Conditional Transit Signal Priority (TSP)
   - Adaptive signal interface with SunRail Positive Train Control
   - Smart parking technology
   - Signal Performance Metrics (SPM)
   - Integrated Corridor Management (ICM)

3. **SmartCommunity** — Multimodal travel information integration
   - Travel time info for driving, bus, rail, rideshare

4. **SunStore** — FDOT's central data repository
   - Master Data Management, Data Fusion, Sensor Fusion
   - Data available to universities, research institutions, businesses
   - Archives data for the ATTAIN program

### TSM&O Program
- FDOT District 5 has a dedicated **TSM&O Implementation Plan** managed with support from **VHB** (consulting firm)
- The TSM&O Consortium holds regular meetings where vendors present (Intelight, Trafficware, Econolite mentioned in meeting minutes)
- District 5 was an early adopter of the statewide TSM&O strategic plan

### Adaptive Signal Control Technology (ASCT) in District 5
From FDOT technical documents and consortium meeting notes:
- **Volusia County** has adaptive signal control projects in construction
- Bids received from **Intelight** and **Trafficware** for adaptive signal systems
- **Econolite** also submitting for Central FL adaptive work
- The district is deploying **Automated Traffic Signal Performance Measures (ATSPM)** via Iteris ClearGuide (see Section 5)

### I-4 Ultimate / Beyond the Ultimate
- **I-4 Ultimate** ($2.3B project) is primarily freeway reconstruction — limited direct smart signal component on arterials
- **I-4 Beyond the Ultimate** extends express toll lanes and adds interchanges
- **Moving I-4 Forward** program has accelerated construction
- These projects affect **arterial signal timing** at ramp termini but don't directly include adaptive arterial signal systems
- **A stretch of I-4 near Orlando's tourism district was found to be THE MOST CONGESTED IN THE U.S.** (2024 INRIX study)

### Key FDOT District 5 Contact
- **Jeremy Dilmore** — leads ATTAIN/Connected Vehicle programs: Jeremy.Dilmore@dot.state.fl.us

**⚡ INSIGHT FOR ALAN:** FDOT District 5 is THE major player. The $65M ATTAIN program is winding down and they'll need next-phase technology partners. The GreenWay program's goal of managing 1,000+ signals with adaptive technology is exactly UpTend's value proposition. The fact that I-4 corridor is the nation's most congested means there's enormous political pressure to show results.

## 6. Florida Statewide Context

### FDOT TSMO Strategic Plan
Florida has one of the **most mature TSM&O programs in the nation**. Key elements:

- **Six primary focus areas:** Connected Vehicle, Management/Deployments, ITS Communications, ITS Software/Architecture, STAMP, Managed Lanes
- Statewide program managed through FDOT's Traffic Engineering and Operations (TEO) Office
- Each of 7 FDOT districts has TSM&O coordinators and implementation plans

### Statewide Arterial Management Program (STAMP)
The STAMP is critical context for Alan:

- **2021 STAMP Action Plan** provides outcome-based actions for arterial management statewide
- **Five Focus Areas:** Field technologies, traffic control strategies, TMC technologies, operations/maintenance, and data/performance
- Promotes **Adaptive Signal Control Technology (ASCT)** deployment
- Promotes **Automated Traffic Signal Performance Measures (ATSPM)**
- Active Arterial Management (AAM) and Integrated Corridor Management (ICM) are key strategies
- **This is the statewide framework** that justifies and funds adaptive signal work at the district level

### Connected Mobility and Technologies Program (CMTP)
FDOT's central office section focused on:
- Connected and Automated Vehicles (CAV) technology deployment
- Infrastructure readiness for CV
- STAMP implementation
- Wrong-Way Driving (WWD) detection
- Managed Lanes programs

### SunTrax Test Facility
- **475-acre facility in Polk County** (between Tampa and Orlando, near Florida Poly)
- **2.25-mile oval test track + 200-acre infield**
- **$150 million investment** by FDOT and Florida's Turnpike Enterprise
- Focus: Autonomous, Connected, and Electric Vehicles (ACES) testing
- Available for **private companies** to test and verify CAV technology
- **Relevant to Alan:** Could be a venue to demonstrate/validate signal optimization technology in a controlled environment

### Florida Turnpike Enterprise CV Deployment
- **50 Roadside Units (RSUs)** along Turnpike Mainline and Beachline
- Applications: Wrong Way Detection, Queue Warning, Curve Speed Warning
- Infrastructure-to-Vehicle (I2V) deployment using C-V2X
- **Operational as of 2024-2025**

### Key Statewide Takeaway
Florida is investing heavily at the state level in connected vehicle infrastructure and arterial management. The STAMP framework creates a **statewide policy mandate** for adaptive signals and ATSPM — agencies can point to STAMP as justification for procurement of adaptive technology.

- As CV penetration grows, **AI signal platforms that can ingest V2X data** will have massive advantage over legacy systems
- **This is where UpTend could differentiate** — building signal optimization that is V2X-native from day one, vs. competitors retrofitting legacy systems

## 10. Gap Analysis & Entry Strategy

### What's NOT Being Done in Central Florida Traffic AI

1. **No widespread adaptive signal control** — Orange County's 600+ signals mostly run fixed time-of-day plans. The Sand Lake/OBT adaptive pilot showed great results but was never scaled.

2. **No AI-driven real-time optimization at scale** — NoTraffic has exactly ONE intersection in Orlando. GridMatrix is analytics-only (15 intersections). There is no city-wide or county-wide AI signal optimization deployed.

3. **No unified cross-jurisdictional signal management** — Orange County, City of Orlando, FDOT, Seminole County, and other municipalities all operate independently. SR 50 (Colonial Drive) crosses Ocoee, Orlando, Winter Garden AND unincorporated Orange County — each with different signal management.

4. **Poor tourist corridor signal management** — International Drive, US 192, Sand Lake Road, and the tourism district have some of the worst congestion in the country, yet no advanced adaptive signal technology.

5. **No predictive/AI signal management for events** — Orange County Convention Center, Kia Center, Camping World Stadium, and theme parks generate massive, predictable traffic spikes that are still managed with manual TMC intervention.

6. **Limited transit signal priority** — LYMMO has basic TSP, but LYNX bus system (serving 2,500 sq miles) has minimal signal priority across its routes.

7. **V2X-native signal optimization doesn't exist yet** — With 33+ intersections equipped with C-V2X RSUs, no one is using that data for real-time optimization.

### Where Are the Biggest Technology Gaps?

| Gap | Severity | Opportunity Size |
|-----|----------|-----------------|
| Adaptive signals at scale (county-wide) | Critical | $10-50M+ |
| AI optimization for tourism corridors | Critical | $5-20M |
| Cross-jurisdictional signal coordination | High | $5-15M |
| Event-based predictive signal management | High | $2-10M |
