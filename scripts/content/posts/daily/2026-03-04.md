## 3. Facebook Group Comment Template (Lake Nona)

**For posts like:** "Anyone know a good plumber?" / "Water under my sink, who do I call?" / "Is this leak serious?"

**Template:**

Not a plumber, but I deal with this stuff a lot. A few things to check before you call anyone:

First, tighten the connections by hand under the sink. Sometimes the slip nuts just loosen over time, especially with garbage disposals.

Second, check if it only leaks when the water is running or all the time. Running only means a supply or drain connection. All the time could mean a supply line under pressure, which is more urgent.

Third, put a dry paper towel around each joint and run the water. The wet spot tells you exactly which connection is the problem.

If it is a simple connection fix, most decent plumbers will handle it for $100 to $200. If someone quotes you $500 for a leaky P trap, keep looking.
