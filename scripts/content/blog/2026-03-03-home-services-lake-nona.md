## The Most Common Home Services Lake Nona Residents Need

Living in Central Florida puts unique demands on your home. The combination of heat, humidity, afternoon storms, and sandy soil means maintenance is not optional. Here are the services Lake Nona homeowners call for most often.

**Landscaping and lawn care** top the list. Florida lawns grow year round, which means mowing, edging, and trimming never really stop. St. Augustine grass is the standard in most Lake Nona neighborhoods, and it needs regular fertilization and pest treatment to stay healthy. Many homeowners also need palm trimming, mulch installation, and irrigation system checks.

**Pressure washing** is another constant. Florida humidity breeds mold and mildew on driveways, sidewalks, pool decks, and exterior walls faster than most people expect. A good pressure wash twice a year keeps your home looking sharp and your HOA off your back.

**Pool maintenance** is huge in Lake Nona. Nearly every new build comes with a pool, and keeping it clean and chemically balanced in Florida heat takes consistent attention. Weekly service is standard for most pool owners.

**Gutter cleaning** tends to get overlooked until there is a problem. With the volume of rain Orlando gets, especially during hurricane season from June through November, clogged gutters can lead to water intrusion, fascia damage, and foundation issues. Most homes need gutter cleaning at least twice a year.

**Handyman and general repair work** covers everything else. Drywall patches, door adjustments, fixture installations, ceiling fan swaps, fence repairs. The list of small jobs that pile up in a home is endless, and most people do not have the time or tools to tackle them all.

## What to Look for When Hiring Home Service Pros in Orlando

Not all service providers are created equal, and a few simple checks can save you real headaches down the road.

**Licensing and insurance matter.** Florida requires contractor licensing for many types of work. Always ask for proof of insurance before anyone starts a job at your home. This protects you if something goes wrong.

**Local reputation beats online ratings.** A company with 500 reviews across 10 cities is not the same as a local pro with 50 reviews from people in your neighborhood. Ask around in your Lake Nona community groups. Word of mouth still carries more weight than algorithm generated star ratings.

**Get clear pricing upfront.** The old model of "we will give you a quote after we come look at it" is a time waster for everyone. Look for service providers who can give you transparent pricing based on the scope of work. No surprises when the invoice arrives.

**Communication is everything.** The number one complaint homeowners have about service providers is not quality of work. It is communication. Missed calls, vague timelines, no follow up. A professional who communicates clearly and shows up when they say they will is worth their weight in gold.

## Seasonal Home Maintenance Tips for Lake Nona Homeowners

Staying ahead of maintenance prevents expensive repairs and keeps your home value strong. Here is a simple seasonal framework that works well for Central Florida.

**Spring (March through May):** Schedule your first pressure wash of the year. Get your irrigation system inspected before the dry season peaks. Check your roof for any damage from winter storms. This is also a great time to refresh mulch beds and handle any landscaping projects before summer heat makes outdoor work brutal.

**Summer (June through August):** Focus on indoor projects. Make sure your AC is serviced and filters are fresh. Stay on top of pool maintenance as usage spikes. Keep gutters clear because afternoon thunderstorms dump serious water volume fast.

**Fall (September through November):** Post hurricane season is the time for a full exterior inspection. Check for any storm damage to your roof, screens, fencing, and exterior paint. Schedule gutter cleaning after the leaves drop. Get your lawn aeration done before the grass goes dormant.

**Winter (December through February):** Handle interior upgrades and repairs while the weather is mild. This is the slowest season for most home service pros, so you may find better availability and pricing.
