## Your Complete Lake Nona Spring Maintenance Checklist

### Exterior Priorities

**Roof and Gutters**
Start from the top down. Inspect your roof for loose or damaged shingles, especially after any recent storms. Clean gutters and downspouts to ensure proper water flow during Florida's rainy season. Look for any signs of pest intrusion or damage.

**Pressure Washing**
Florida's humidity and occasional pollen can leave your home's exterior looking less than pristine. A thorough pressure washing of siding, driveways, walkways, and outdoor surfaces restores that fresh, clean appearance while preventing mold and mildew buildup.

**HVAC System Care**
Your air conditioning system works hard in Central Florida. Replace or clean filters, clear debris from around outdoor units, and schedule professional maintenance to ensure efficient operation when the heat arrives.

### Interior Essentials

**Plumbing Check**
Inspect faucets, toilets, and visible pipes for leaks. Check water pressure and ensure all fixtures are functioning properly. Small leaks can quickly become big problems in Florida's humid environment.

**Electrical Safety**
Test GFCI outlets, especially in bathrooms and outdoor areas. Check that all switches and outlets are working correctly, and ensure your home's electrical system is ready for increased air conditioning use.

**Deep Cleaning and Organization**
Spring is perfect for a thorough cleaning and decluttering. This is also an ideal time to assess what items you no longer need and clear space for better organization.

### Seasonal Preparations

**Storm Readiness**
Review your storm preparedness plan. Check that flashlights work, batteries are fresh, and emergency supplies are stocked. Trim any tree branches that might threaten your roof or windows during severe weather.

**Pest Prevention**
Florida's year round growing season means pest control is always important. Seal gaps around windows and doors, eliminate standing water sources, and address any areas where pests might find entry points.
