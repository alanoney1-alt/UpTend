# The Complete Guide to Pressure Washing Your Lake Nona Home

*Published March 3, 2026*

Lake Nona homeowners know that keeping your property looking pristine in Central Florida takes work. Between our year round humidity, frequent afternoon storms, and that ever present pollen, your home's exterior surfaces take quite a beating. If you've been wondering whether pressure washing is right for your Lake Nona property, this guide covers everything you need to know.

## Why Lake Nona Properties Need Regular Pressure Washing

Living in one of Orlando's most desirable communities comes with unique challenges. The Medical City area's lush landscaping and proximity to Lake Nona itself create the perfect environment for:

**Mold and Mildew Growth**
Our humid subtropical climate creates ideal conditions for organic growth on exterior surfaces. You'll notice it first on north facing walls and areas with limited sun exposure.

**Pollen Accumulation** 
Those beautiful oak trees and flowering plants that make Lake Nona so scenic? They coat everything in a fine yellow dust from February through May.

**Algae and Lichen**
The combination of moisture and warmth allows algae to thrive on driveways, walkways, and pool decks throughout the year.

## What Surfaces Need Pressure Washing?

**Your Home's Exterior**
Vinyl siding, stucco, and brick all benefit from annual cleaning. The key is using the right pressure settings. Too much force can damage softer materials like stucco, which is common in many Lake Nona neighborhoods.

**Driveways and Walkways**
Concrete surfaces collect oil stains, dirt, and organic growth. Regular cleaning not only improves curb appeal but prevents permanent staining.

**Pool Areas**
Your lanai screening and pool deck need special attention. These areas see constant moisture and are prime spots for algae growth.

**Outdoor Living Spaces**
With Lake Nona's focus on outdoor entertaining, your patios, pergolas, and outdoor kitchens deserve professional attention.

## The Right Approach for Different Materials

**Soft Washing vs High Pressure**
Not everything needs maximum pressure. Delicate surfaces like roof shingles or painted wood require soft washing techniques with specialized cleaning solutions.

**Concrete and Stone**
These hard surfaces can handle higher pressure settings to remove deep stains and embedded dirt.

**Wood Surfaces**
Decks and fences need careful handling to avoid damage to the wood grain.

## When to Schedule Pressure Washing

**Best Times of Year**
Fall and early spring offer ideal conditions. You'll avoid the intense summer heat and afternoon thunderstorms that make outdoor work challenging.

**Before and After Events**
Many Lake Nona residents schedule cleaning before hosting outdoor gatherings or after major storms.

**Preventive Maintenance**
Annual cleaning prevents buildup that becomes harder and more expensive to remove over time.

## Choosing Professional vs DIY

**Equipment Considerations**
Professional grade equipment produces better results than rental units. More importantly, experienced technicians know which techniques work best for different materials.

**Safety Factors**
Pressure washing involves risks from both the equipment and cleaning chemicals. Two story homes require ladder work that many homeowners prefer to leave to professionals.

**Time Investment**
A typical Lake Nona home takes 4 to 6 hours to pressure wash properly, including preparation and cleanup time.

## What to Expect from Professional Service

**Property Assessment**
A good service starts with evaluating your specific needs. Different areas of your property may require different approaches.

**Preparation Work**
This includes covering plants, moving outdoor furniture, and ensuring proper water flow management.

**Post Cleaning Care**
Professional services include proper disposal of wastewater and restoration of your landscaping areas.

## Environmental Considerations in Lake Nona

Our community values environmental stewardship. Look for services that:

- Use biodegradable cleaning products when possible
- Manage runoff to protect our lakes and waterways
- Follow local environmental guidelines

## Getting Ready for Professional Pressure Washing

**Clear the Area**
Move patio furniture, potted plants, and decorative items away from work areas.

**Close Windows and Doors**
Protect your home's interior from overspray and moisture.

**Trim Vegetation**
Cut back plants growing close to your home's exterior walls.

## Investment in Your Property Value

Regular pressure washing maintains your home's curb appeal, which directly impacts property value. In a competitive market like Lake Nona, maintaining your exterior appearance helps protect your investment.
