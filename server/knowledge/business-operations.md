## Daily Operations

### Morning Routine Checklist for Home Service Business Owners

**5:30-6:00 AM - Pre-Day Setup**
- [ ] Check weather forecast for all job locations
- [ ] Review daily schedule and confirm all appointments
- [ ] Check overnight messages/emails for urgent requests
- [ ] Verify technician assignments and availability
- [ ] Load vehicles with necessary tools/materials for the day
- [ ] Review previous day's incomplete jobs or callbacks

**6:00-7:00 AM - Team Coordination**
- [ ] Brief team on daily priorities and special instructions
- [ ] Distribute route sheets and customer contact information
- [ ] Confirm first appointments with customers (text/call)
- [ ] Check inventory levels and emergency backup supplies
- [ ] Ensure all safety equipment is present and functional
- [ ] Update GPS systems with optimal routes

**Throughout the Day**
- [ ] Monitor job progress via field management software
- [ ] Handle incoming calls and emergency requests
- [ ] Update customers on arrival times and job progress
- [ ] Review and approve job completion photos
- [ ] Process payments and update job status
- [ ] Dispatch changes and route adjustments as needed

**End of Day - 5:00-6:00 PM**
- [ ] Reconcile daily revenue vs. estimates
- [ ] Review and send invoices for completed jobs
- [ ] Update customer follow-up schedules
- [ ] Plan next day's routes and assignments
- [ ] Restock vehicles and equipment
- [ ] Document any equipment issues or maintenance needs
- [ ] Update job costing and profit margin tracking

### Route Optimization - Reduce Drive Time = More Jobs/Day

**The 20% Rule:** Optimizing routes can reduce drive time by 20%, translating to 1-2 additional jobs per day per technician.

**Manual Route Planning:**
1. **Geographic Clustering:** Group jobs within 5-mile radius
2. **Time-Based Sequencing:** Start with longest jobs first thing in morning
3. **Traffic Consideration:** Avoid rush hours (7-9 AM, 4-6 PM) for non-emergency work
4. **Fuel Efficiency:** Plan clockwise or counter-clockwise circles, not criss-crossing

**Software Route Optimization Features:**
- **Real-time traffic integration** (saves 15-30 minutes daily per tech)
- **Automatic re-routing** for emergency calls
- **Customer preference matching** (morning/afternoon availability)
- **Technician skill-set matching** to job requirements

**Route Optimization ROI Example:**
- Baseline: 4 jobs/day with 2 hours drive time
- Optimized: 5 jobs/day with 1.5 hours drive time
- Additional revenue: $200-400/day per technician
- Annual impact: $50,000-100,000 per technician

### Job Documentation Standards

**Before/After Photos - Required for Every Job**
- Wide-angle shot showing full work area
- Close-up of specific problem area
- Progress photos for multi-day jobs
- Final completion shots from multiple angles
- Time-stamp all photos (built into most field service apps)

**Work Order Documentation Requirements:**
1. **Arrival time and conditions found**
2. **Materials used (quantities and costs)**
3. **Labor hours by task**
4. **Any additional work identified**
5. **Customer approvals for change orders**
6. **Safety protocols followed**
7. **Disposal/cleanup notes**
8. **Customer signature for completion**

**Change Order Process:**
```
1. Identify additional work needed
2. Take photo documentation of issue
3. Provide written estimate with materials and labor
4. Get customer approval (signature/text approval)
5. Document approval in job notes
6. Complete additional work
7. Update final invoice with change order line items
```

## Scaling Your Business

### When to Hire Your First Employee vs Stay Solo

**Solo Operator Capacity Limits:**
- **Physical:** 4-6 jobs per day maximum
- **Revenue:** $150,000-300,000 annual ceiling
- **Time:** Working 60+ hours per week consistently
- **Opportunity Cost:** Turning away 20+ hours of work weekly

**Financial Signals to Hire:**
- Consistent monthly revenue >$20,000 for 3+ months
- Backlog of 2+ weeks for non-emergency work
- Declining job completion quality due to rushed schedule
- Customer complaints about scheduling delays

**The First Hire Decision Matrix:**

| Factor | Stay Solo | Hire Technician |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Monthly Revenue | <$15,000 | >$20,000 |
| Weekly Hours | <55 hours | >60 hours |
| Job Backlog | <1 week | >2 weeks |
| Growth Rate | <10%/month | >15%/month |
| Cash Flow | 1-2 months | 3+ months |

**First Hire Should Be:** Another technician, NOT administrative staff
**Reason:** Administrative tasks can be automated/outsourced cheaper than skilled labor

### Adding Service Verticals - Which Ones Pair Well

**Natural Service Combinations:**

**Cleaning + Organizing:**
- House cleaning customers need organization help
- Shared tools: cleaning supplies, transportation
- Cross-sell rate: 35-45%
- Revenue increase per customer: 40-60%

**Handyman + Painting:**
- Repair work often requires paint touch-ups
- Shared tools: ladders, drop cloths, basic tools
- Cross-sell rate: 50-65%
- Higher-margin paint jobs offset lower-margin handyman work

**Landscaping + Pressure Washing:**
- Seasonal complement (landscaping spring/summer, pressure washing fall/winter)
- Shared customer base and marketing channels
- Equipment synergies: trucks, trailers, water access
- Year-round revenue stream

**Junk Removal + Moving:**
- Similar customer base (relocating, downsizing)
- Shared equipment: trucks, dollies, labor
- Complementary timing: moving creates junk removal needs

**Pool Cleaning + General Maintenance:**
- Pool customers often need handyman services
- Regular customer relationship enables upselling
- Premium customer base willing to pay for convenience

**HVAC + Electrical + Plumbing (The Triple Threat):**
- Highest barriers to entry (licensing required)
- Highest profit margins (25-40%)
- Natural cross-selling opportunities
- Significant startup investment required ($50,000-150,000)

### Complaint Handling - The HEARD Method

**H - HEAR:** Listen completely without interrupting
- Let customer express full concern
- Take notes on specific issues
- Ask clarifying questions
- Repeat back what you heard to confirm understanding

**E - EMPATHIZE:** Show genuine understanding
- "I understand why you'd be frustrated"
- "That's not the experience we want for our customers"
- "I would feel the same way in your situation"
- Avoid defensive responses or excuses

**A - APOLOGIZE:** Take responsibility appropriately
- "I'm sorry this happened"
- "We didn't meet our standards"
- "This isn't acceptable"
- Apologize for the experience, not necessarily admitting fault

**R - RESOLVE:** Take action to fix the issue
- Offer specific solutions with timelines

## Common Mistakes

### Pricing and Financial Mistakes

**Underpricing to Win Jobs (The Death Spiral):**
- **Problem:** Competing solely on price leads to unsustainable margins
- **Reality:** Price competition = race to bottom = business failure
- **Solution:** Compete on value, quality, and customer service
- **Recovery:** Gradually increase prices 15-25% over 6 months while improving service quality

**Not Tracking True Job Costs:**
- **Hidden Costs:** Travel time, fuel, equipment wear, admin time
- **Real Cost Example:** $200 quoted job actually costs $180 when all factors included
- **Solution:** Track all time from departure to return, including materials pickup
- **Tool:** Use field service software to automatically calculate true job costs

**Mixing Personal and Business Finances:**
- **IRS Red Flag:** Audit risk increases significantly
- **Cash Flow Confusion:** Cannot track business profitability accurately
- **Solution:** Separate business bank account from day 1, use business credit card only for business expenses
- **Recovery:** Clean up finances retroactively with bookkeeper/accountant

### Operational Mistakes

**No Written Contracts/Agreements:**
- **Legal Risk:** No protection against scope creep, non-payment
- **Customer Confusion:** Mismatched expectations lead to disputes
- **Solution:** Written estimate/contract for every job over $100
- **Must Include:** Scope of work, materials, timeline, payment terms, warranty

**Not Having Insurance:**
- **Single Accident Risk:** Could bankrupt business and personal assets
- **Customer Requirements:** Many customers require proof of insurance
- **Legal Requirements:** Required for licensing in most trades
- **Solution:** General liability minimum $1M, workers' comp if employees

**Ignoring Online Reviews:**
- **Impact:** 87% of customers read reviews before hiring
- **Compounding Effect:** Negative reviews reduce future business significantly
- **Solution:** Monitor all platforms, respond professionally to negative reviews
- **Recovery Strategy:** Focus on earning 5-10 positive reviews to dilute negative ones

### Scaling Mistakes

**Hiring Too Fast:**
- **Cash Flow Crisis:** Payroll obligations before revenue increases
- **Quality Control:** Cannot properly train multiple new employees simultaneously
- **Solution:** Hire one technician at a time, ensure profitability before next hire

**Hiring Too Slow:**
- **Opportunity Cost:** Missing revenue due to capacity constraints
- **Owner Burnout:** Working 70+ hours per week unsustainable long-term
- **Customer Service:** Long delays lead to lost customers
- **Solution:** Track capacity utilization, hire when consistently above 80%

**Not Building Systems:**
- **Owner Dependency:** Business cannot operate without owner presence
- **Inconsistent Quality:** Different results depending on who performs work
- **Training Difficulties:** Cannot effectively onboard new employees
- **Solution:** Document all procedures, create checklists, implement training programs

### Marketing Mistakes

**Inconsistent Branding:**
- **Customer Confusion:** Different messages across platforms reduce trust
- **Professional Image:** Inconsistent appearance reduces perceived credibility
- **Solution:** Develop brand guidelines, ensure consistency across all materials

**Neglecting Google My Business:**
- **Local Search Impact:** 60% of local searches go to Google My Business
- **Competitive Disadvantage:** Competitors with better profiles win more business
- **Solution:** Complete profile 100%, post regularly, manage reviews actively

**No Marketing Budget/Plan:**
- **Feast or Famine:** Irregular lead flow creates cash flow problems
- **Emergency Marketing:** Desperation marketing rarely produces good ROI
- **Solution:** Allocate 5-10% of revenue to marketing, develop 12-month plan

### Customer Service Mistakes

**Poor Communication:**
- **Customer Anxiety:** Lack of updates creates customer stress and complaints
- **Review Impact:** Communication issues mentioned in 40% of negative reviews
- **Solution:** Proactive communication at every step, automated update systems

**Not Following Up:**
- **Missed Opportunities:** 20-30% of customers need additional services within 6 months
- **Referral Loss:** Satisfied customers forget to refer without reminders
- **Solution:** Automated follow-up sequence, regular check-ins with past customers

**Overpromising and Under-delivering:**
- **Expectation Gap:** Creates disappointed customers even with good work
- **Review Impact:** Significantly increases negative review probability
- **Solution:** Conservative estimates, exceed expectations when possible
