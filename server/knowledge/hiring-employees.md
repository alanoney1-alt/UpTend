## When to Hire Your First Employee {#when-to-hire}

### Revenue Signals
- **Monthly Revenue Threshold**: $15,000-20,000 recurring monthly revenue for service-based businesses
- **Annual Revenue Target**: $180,000-240,000 annual gross revenue before considering first hire
- **Profit Margin**: Maintain at least 25-30% profit margin before adding payroll costs
- **Cash Flow**: 3-6 months of employee wages in cash reserves
- **Growth Rate**: 15-20% month-over-month growth for 3+ consecutive months

### Capacity Signals
- **Booking Lead Times**: Scheduling jobs 2-3 weeks out consistently
- **Daily Job Volume**: Running 8-12 service calls daily as solo operator
- **Weekend Work**: Working 6-7 days per week regularly to meet demand
- **Customer Wait Times**: Customers waiting more than 5-7 days for service
- **Geographic Expansion**: Ready to serve additional ZIP codes but lack capacity

### Time Signals
- **Work Week Hours**: Consistently working 60+ hours per week
- **Administrative Burden**: Spending 20+ hours weekly on non-revenue activities
- **Customer Service**: Unable to answer calls promptly during service hours
- **Quality Concerns**: Rushing through jobs to meet demand
- **Personal Life Impact**: No time for family, health, or business development

### Break-Even Analysis for First Hire
**Example: Orlando Handyman Business**
- New Helper at $17/hour × 40 hours = $680 weekly gross
- Payroll taxes (FICA, unemployment, workers comp) = $150 weekly
- Total weekly cost = $830
- Monthly cost = $3,320
- **Revenue needed to break even**: $5,000-6,000 additional monthly revenue

## Employee vs 1099 Contractor Classification {#employee-vs-contractor}

### IRS 3-Factor Test
**1. Behavioral Control**
- Who controls what work is done and how it's done?
- Does the business provide training on procedures?
- Are specific instructions given for task completion?
- **Employee**: Business controls work methods and timing
- **Contractor**: Worker controls how work is performed

**2. Financial Control**
- Who provides tools and equipment?
- How is the worker paid - hourly/salary vs project-based?
- Does worker have opportunity for profit/loss?
- **Employee**: Company provides tools, pays regular wages
- **Contractor**: Uses own tools, paid per project, can profit/lose

**3. Relationship Type**
- Written contracts describing relationship?
- Employee benefits (insurance, PTO, retirement)?
- Permanency of relationship?
- **Employee**: Benefits, ongoing relationship, integral to business
- **Contractor**: No benefits, temporary projects, supplemental to business

### Florida ABC Test (More Restrictive)
For unemployment insurance purposes, Florida uses the ABC test. Worker is contractor ONLY if ALL three conditions are met:

**A) Absence of Control**
- Worker free from control and direction in performance
- No supervision over work methods or timing
- Worker sets own schedule and procedures

**B) Business Outside Usual Course**
- Work performed is outside usual course of hiring entity's business
- Example: Accounting service for HVAC company = outside usual course
- Example: HVAC tech for HVAC company = within usual course

**C) Customarily Engaged**
- Worker customarily engaged in independently established trade
- Has business license, insurance, multiple clients
- Advertises services independently

### Misclassification Penalties
**Federal (IRS)**
- $50 per W-2 not filed
- 1.5% of wages for unpaid income tax withholding
- 40% of unpaid FICA taxes
- Interest and penalties on unpaid amounts

**Florida State**
- Unpaid unemployment taxes plus 10% penalty
- $1,000 per misclassified worker per quarter
- Criminal penalties for willful violations
- Department of Revenue audit triggers

**Workers' Compensation**
- Retroactive premiums for entire policy period
- 50% penalty on unpaid premiums
- $1,000-5,000 fine per employee per day without coverage
- Criminal charges for willful non-compliance

### Construction Industry Special Rules
**Florida Statute 440.02**: Independent contractors NOT allowed in construction industry for workers' compensation purposes. All construction workers must be classified as employees regardless of IRS status.

**Affected Trades:**
- Roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical
- Flooring, painting, drywall, tile
- Landscaping hardscaping
- Pool construction and major repairs

## Job Descriptions and Roles {#job-descriptions}

### Field Technician (Entry Level)
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Perform routine service calls under supervision
- Use basic tools and equipment safely
- Complete digital work orders and photos
- Maintain vehicle cleanliness and organization
- Follow safety protocols and company procedures

**Requirements:**
- High school diploma or equivalent
- Clean driving record (3 years)
- Ability to lift 50 pounds regularly
- Basic tool knowledge and mechanical aptitude
- Professional appearance and communication skills
- Drug screening and background check clearance

**Performance Metrics:**
- Jobs completed per day (target: 6-8)
- Customer satisfaction scores (target: 4.5+/5.0)
- Safety incidents (target: zero)
- Revenue per technician (target: $800-1,200/day)
- Callback rate (target: <5%)

### Lead Technician (Experienced)
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Manage complex repairs and installations
- Mentor and train junior technicians
- Handle difficult customer situations
- Estimate and sell additional services
- Ensure quality control on all jobs

**Additional Qualifications:**
- 2+ years relevant experience
- Trade certifications preferred
- Upselling and sales skills
- Leadership and training abilities
- Problem-solving and diagnostic skills

**Performance Metrics:**
- Average ticket size (target: $350-500)
- Upsell conversion rate (target: 25-35%)
- Training effectiveness of junior staff
- Customer retention rate (target: 80%+)
- Technical certifications maintained

### Office Manager
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Manage scheduling and dispatch operations
- Handle customer service and complaints
- Process invoicing and payments
- Coordinate with field technicians
- Maintain CRM and business systems

**Requirements:**
- Associates degree or equivalent experience
- 2+ years customer service experience
- Proficiency in office software and CRM systems
- Excellent communication and organizational skills
- Problem-solving and multitasking abilities

**Key Systems:**
- ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber CRM
- QuickBooks accounting software
- Google Workspace or Microsoft Office
- Scheduling and routing software
- Review management platforms

### Dispatcher/Coordinator
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Optimize daily technician routes
- Manage emergency and priority calls
- Communicate with customers about ETAs
- Track technician progress and productivity
- Handle supply and parts ordering

**Performance Metrics:**
- Route efficiency (minimized drive time)
- First-call resolution rate
- Customer wait time (target: <24 hours)
- Technician utilization rate (target: 85%+)
- Emergency response time (target: <2 hours)

### Sales Estimator
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Conduct in-home consultations and estimates
- Present service options and pricing
- Follow up on proposals and close sales
- Build relationships with potential customers
- Track leads through sales pipeline

**Compensation Structure:**
- Base salary: $35,000-45,000
- Commission: 3-8% of gross sales
- Bonus for monthly targets
- Total earning potential: $55,000-85,000

**Key Skills:**
- Consultative selling approach
- Technical knowledge of services offered
- CRM management and lead nurturing
- Presentation and communication skills
- Objection handling and closing techniques

### Apprentice/Helper
**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Assist lead technicians with jobs
- Load/unload equipment and materials
- Perform basic cleaning and prep work
- Learn company procedures and safety protocols
- Complete training modules and certifications

**Career Development Path:**
- Month 1-3: Basic helper tasks, safety training
- Month 4-6: Simple repairs under supervision
- Month 7-12: Independent basic jobs, advanced training
- Year 2+: Technician promotion with pay increase

## Screening and Interview Process {#screening-process}

### Application Screening
**Red Flags:**
- Gaps in employment history >6 months
- Multiple jobs lasting <1 year
- Lack of relevant experience or training
- Poor spelling/grammar in application
- Incomplete contact information

**Positive Indicators:**
- Stable employment history
- Relevant certifications or training
- Military service or trade school education
- Volunteer work or community involvement
- References from supervisors (not just peers)

### Phone Screen (15-20 minutes)
**Key Questions:**
1. "Walk me through your relevant experience in home services"
2. "What interests you about working for our company specifically?"
3. "Are you comfortable with physical work and varying schedules?"
4. "What are your salary expectations and availability?"
5. "Do you have reliable transportation and clean driving record?"

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Professional communication skills
- Enthusiasm and genuine interest
- Realistic expectations about role
- Scheduling and transportation logistics
- Cultural fit indicators

### In-Person Interview
**Structure (45-60 minutes):**
- Company overview and culture (10 minutes)
- Technical skills assessment (15 minutes)
- Behavioral interview questions (20 minutes)
- Candidate questions and next steps (10 minutes)

**Behavioral Questions:**
- "Describe a time you dealt with an upset customer"
- "How do you prioritize when you have multiple urgent tasks?"
- "Tell me about a mistake you made and how you handled it"
- "Describe a time you went above and beyond for a customer"
- "How do you stay motivated during repetitive or boring tasks?"

**Technical Assessment:**
- Basic tool identification and usage
- Problem-solving scenarios relevant to role
- Safety knowledge and awareness
- Reading comprehension (work orders, instructions)
- Basic math skills (measurements, calculations)

### Ride-Along Assessment
**Purpose:**
- Observe candidate in actual work environment
- Assess customer interaction skills
- Evaluate safety awareness and practices
- Test punctuality and professionalism
- Gauge learning ability and coachability

**Duration:** Half-day (4 hours) with experienced technician

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Attitude and enthusiasm
- Ability to follow instructions
- Customer interaction quality
- Safety protocol adherence
- Tool handling and mechanical aptitude

### Background Check (Certn Recommended)
**Certn Background Screening:**
- Cost: $15-25 per check
- Turnaround: 1-2 business days
- Integration with major HR systems
- Compliance with FCRA requirements
- Real-time status updates

**Standard Check Includes:**
- Criminal history search (7-10 years)
- Sex offender registry check
- Social Security verification
- Address history verification
- Professional reference verification

**Enhanced Check (for senior roles):**
- Credit history review
- Education verification
- Professional license verification
- Previous employment verification
- Motor vehicle record check

### Drug Screening
**Testing Methods:**
- Urine test: $25-50, results in 24-48 hours
- Hair follicle: $75-100, results in 3-5 days
- Instant tests: $15-25, results in 15 minutes

**Recommended Panels:**
- DOT 5-panel for drivers (marijuana, cocaine, amphetamines, opiates, PCP)
- 10-panel for all other positions (adds benzodiazepines, barbiturates, methadone, propoxyphene, Quaaludes)

**Policy Considerations:**
- Pre-employment testing required
- Reasonable suspicion testing allowed
- Post-accident testing mandatory
- Random testing for safety-sensitive positions
- Medical marijuana considerations under Florida law

### Motor Vehicle Record Check
**Required for all driving positions:**
- Cost: $7-15 per MVR report
- Check 3-5 years of driving history
- Automatic disqualifiers: DUI, license suspension, more than 3 moving violations
- Annual re-checks for all drivers

**Insurance Requirements:**
- Commercial auto policy covers business use
- Personal auto may not cover business driving
- Driver must be added to company policy
- Additional premium: $200-500 per driver annually

## Florida Employment Law Requirements {#florida-legal}

### Workers' Compensation Insurance
**Coverage Requirements:**
- **Construction businesses**: 1+ employee requires coverage
- **Non-construction businesses**: 4+ regular employees OR 6+ agricultural workers
- **Seasonal workers**: 12+ working >30 days per season or >45 total days per year
- **Out-of-state employers**: Must notify insurance carrier of Florida work

**Premium Calculation:**
- Based on NCCI (National Council on Compensation Insurance) rates
- Varies by job classification and risk level
- Experience modification factor applied based on claims history

### Minimum Wage Compliance
**Current Rates (Effective September 30, 2025):**
- General minimum wage: $14.00/hour
- Tipped employees: $10.98/hour (plus tips to reach minimum)
- Annual adjustment: September 30 each year based on CPI
- Path to $15/hour: Scheduled to reach $15.00 by September 30, 2026

**Overtime Requirements:**
- Time-and-a-half for hours over 40 per work week
- No daily overtime requirement
- Salaried employees must earn minimum $684/week to be exempt
- Accurate time tracking required
- Premium pay for emergency and holiday work

- Required information: SSN, name, address, wages, start date

- Quarterly wage reporting required
