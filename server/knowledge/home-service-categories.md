# Home Service Categories - Comprehensive Guide

*Complete coverage of 30 major home service categories with Florida-specific licensing, Orlando pricing, and business insights*

## HVAC

**Description**
Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning services are essential in Florida's hot, humid climate. HVAC contractors install, repair, and maintain cooling systems, with AC being the primary focus in Central Florida.

**Common Services**
- Air conditioning repair and maintenance
- AC unit replacement and installation
- Ductwork cleaning and sealing
- Thermostat installation and programming
- Indoor air quality services
- Heat pump installation (rare in Orlando)
- Emergency AC service calls

**Florida Licensing**
State license required through DBPR (Department of Business and Professional Regulation). Air Conditioning Contractor license (CAC) covers installation and repair of central AC systems. Mechanical Contractor license (CFC) covers broader HVAC work. Requires passing trade and business/finance exams, 4 years experience or apprenticeship, insurance, and financial responsibility.

**Startup Costs**
$15,000 - $50,000 range. Breakdown: Licensing/bonding ($2,000), commercial vehicle ($8,000-$20,000), basic tools/equipment ($3,000-$8,000), insurance ($2,000-$5,000), initial inventory ($2,000-$5,000), marketing ($1,000-$3,000), business formation/legal ($1,000-$2,000).

**Orlando FL Pricing**
- Basic AC repair: $200-$400
- Major repair (compressor/refrigerant): $1,000-$3,000
- AC unit replacement (3-ton): $3,800-$5,000
- Maintenance/tune-up: $100-$200
- Emergency service call: $150-$300
- Duct cleaning: $300-$700

**Seasonality**
Peak season: May through September (extreme demand). Moderate: March-April, October. Slow season: November-February. Summer emergency calls command premium pricing. Pre-season maintenance surge in March-April.

**Key Equipment**
- Refrigerant gauges and manifolds
- Vacuum pumps and recovery machines
- Multimeters and electrical testing equipment
- Pipe cutters and brazing torch
- Leak detectors and charging scales
- Service truck with ladder racks
- Safety equipment (safety glasses, gloves)

**Profit Margins**
Service calls: 60-80%. Parts markup: 100-300%. New installations: 30-50%. Maintenance contracts: 70-85%. Emergency services during peak summer: up to 100%.

**Common Customer Problems**
AC not cooling, unit won't turn on, strange noises, high electric bills, poor airflow, frozen coils, thermostat issues, refrigerant leaks, uneven cooling between rooms.

**DIY vs Pro**
DIY: Filter changes, thermostat battery replacement, basic cleaning around outdoor unit, checking circuit breakers.
Pro required: Refrigerant work (EPA certified), electrical connections, ductwork, unit installation, diagnosis of complex problems. Florida law requires licensed technician for most AC work.

## Plumbing

**Description**
Plumbing services address water supply, drainage, and sewer issues in residential and commercial properties. Florida's sandy soil, older homes, and hard water create unique plumbing challenges requiring specialized knowledge.

**Common Services**
- Drain cleaning and unclogging
- Leak detection and repair
- Toilet and faucet installation/repair
- Water heater service and replacement
- Sewer line inspection and cleaning
- Pipe repair and repiping
- Emergency plumbing services

**Florida Licensing**
State Plumbing Contractor license required through DBPR. Category includes Plumbing Contractor (CFC) for full plumbing work. Requires trade exam, business/finance exam, 4 years experience, insurance coverage, and demonstrated financial responsibility. Some municipalities have additional local requirements.

**Startup Costs**
$10,000 - $35,000 range. Breakdown: Licensing/bonding ($1,500), service vehicle ($8,000-$18,000), plumbing tools/equipment ($2,000-$6,000), camera/snake equipment ($2,000-$8,000), insurance ($1,500-$3,000), initial parts inventory ($1,500-$4,000), marketing ($1,000), business setup ($500-$1,000).

**Orlando FL Pricing**
- Service call/diagnosis: $100-$150
- Drain cleaning: $150-$400
- Toilet repair: $150-$300
- Faucet installation: $200-$400
- Water heater replacement: $1,200-$2,500
- Pipe repair: $200-$600
- Sewer line cleaning: $300-$800
- Average job cost: $146-$378

**Seasonality**
Fairly consistent year-round. Slight increase during hurricane season (June-November) for storm damage. Holiday periods (Thanksgiving, Christmas) see more emergency calls. Summer months may see increased outdoor pipe issues.

**Key Equipment**
- Pipe wrenches and channel locks
- Drain snakes and augers
- Pipe cutters and threading tools
- Propane torch for soldering
- Pipe cameras for inspection
- Wet/dry vacuum for cleanups
- Pipe locating equipment

**Profit Margins**
Service calls: 65-75%. Emergency services: 80-100%. Parts markup: 100-200%. Fixture installations: 50-70%. Major projects (repiping): 35-55%.

**Common Customer Problems**
Clogged drains, running toilets, dripping faucets, low water pressure, water heater issues, sewer backups, pipe leaks, garbage disposal problems, outdoor faucet repairs.

**DIY vs Pro**
DIY: Simple drain snaking, toilet flapper replacement, basic faucet aerator cleaning, caulking around tubs.
Pro required: Major pipe work, gas lines, sewer connections, water heater installation, anything requiring permits. Florida plumbing code compliance mandatory for most work.

## Electrical

**Description**
Electrical contractors provide installation, repair, and maintenance of electrical systems in homes and businesses. Florida's hurricane risk and older housing stock create high demand for electrical upgrades and storm preparation.

**Common Services**
- Electrical panel upgrades and replacement
- Outlet and switch installation/repair
- Ceiling fan installation
- Whole-house rewiring
- Electrical troubleshooting
- Generator installation and hookup
- Code compliance upgrades

**Florida Licensing**
State Electrical Contractor license required through DBPR via Electrical Contractors' Licensing Board (ECLB). Certified Electrical Contractor (EC) for unlimited work, or Registered Electrical Contractor (ER) for limited scope. Requires electrical trade exam, business/finance exam, experience requirements, insurance, and surety bond.

**Startup Costs**
$12,000 - $40,000 range. Breakdown: Licensing/examination/bonding ($2,500), service vehicle with tool storage ($10,000-$20,000), electrical tools and meters ($2,000-$6,000), wire/materials inventory ($2,000-$5,000), insurance ($1,500-$4,000), marketing ($1,000-$2,000), business formation ($500-$1,500).

**Orlando FL Pricing**
- New outlet installation: $100-$200
- Panel upgrade (200-amp): $2,000-$3,500
- Ceiling fan installation: $150-$300
- Circuit breaker replacement: $150-$400
- Whole-house rewiring: $8,000-$15,000
- Generator hookup: $1,500-$4,000
- Service call/diagnosis: $100-$200

**Seasonality**
Hurricane season (June-November) drives generator and electrical prep work. Post-storm demand spikes dramatically. Winter months slower but steady for indoor projects. Spring renovation season increases panel upgrade requests.

**Key Equipment**
- Digital multimeters and voltage testers
- Wire strippers and electrical pliers
- Conduit benders and fish tapes
- Oscilloscope for complex diagnosis
- Insulated hand tools (required)
- Ladder and safety equipment
- Circuit analyzers and load testers

**Profit Margins**
Service calls: 70-85%. Material markup: 100-250%. Panel upgrades: 40-60%. Emergency/storm work: 100-150%. Generator installations: 50-70%.

**Common Customer Problems**
Tripped breakers, outlets not working, flickering lights, burning electrical smells, inadequate power for appliances, old panels, GFCI issues, storm damage.

**DIY vs Pro**
DIY: Changing light bulbs, resetting GFCI outlets, replacing light switch covers.
Pro required: All wiring work, panel work, new circuits, anything requiring permits. Florida electrical code strictly enforced - unlicensed work creates safety hazards and insurance issues.

## Roofing

**Startup Costs**
$20,000 - $60,000 range. Breakdown: Licensing/bonding ($3,000), commercial truck/trailer ($15,000-$30,000), roofing tools/equipment ($3,000-$8,000), safety equipment/harnesses ($1,500-$3,000), insurance (significant - $3,000-$8,000), material credit lines ($2,000-$5,000), marketing ($2,000-$5,000).

**Orlando FL Pricing**

- Per square foot: $5-$8 asphalt, $8-$15 metal
- Roof repair: $300-$1,500
- Gutter cleaning: $150-$300
- Roof inspection: $200-$500
- Emergency tarping: $400-$1,200
- Insurance claim work varies significantly

**Seasonality**
Hurricane season (June-November) creates massive demand spikes. Post-storm periods see 6-month+ backlogs. Winter months are prime for proactive replacements. Spring brings pre-hurricane prep work. Weather dependency affects daily scheduling.

**Key Equipment**
- Safety harnesses and fall protection
- Roofing nailers and compressors
- Ladders and roof jacks
- Tear-off tools and shovels
- Measuring tools and chalk lines
- Tarps and emergency materials

**Profit Margins**
Insurance work: 40-70%. Cash jobs: 50-80%. Emergency/storm work: 70-100%. Maintenance contracts: 60-80%. Material markups: 50-100%.

**Common Customer Problems**
Roof leaks, missing/damaged shingles, storm damage, age-related deterioration, insurance requirements for replacement, gutter problems, poor previous workmanship.

**DIY vs Pro**
DIY: Gutter cleaning (single-story), minor caulking, debris removal from roof surface.
Pro required: Any structural work, steep roofs, multi-story work, insurance claims, warranty work. Safety risks and liability make professional service essential for most roofing work.
