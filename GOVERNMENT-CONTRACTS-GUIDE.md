## 1. CERTIFICATION PROGRAMS (How to Get Certified)

### **SBA 8(a) Business Development Program**
**Administering Agency:** U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA)

**What it gives you:**
- Sole-source contracts up to $4.5 million (non-manufacturing) / $7 million (manufacturing)
- Set-aside contracts (competitive among 8(a) firms only)
- 9-year program participation
- Business development assistance and mentoring
- Price evaluation preference in full and open competitions

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be a small business per SBA size standards
- At least 51% owned/controlled by socially and economically disadvantaged U.S. citizens
- Owner personal net worth ≤ $850,000 (excluding primary residence and business equity)
- Adjusted gross income ≤ $400,000 (3-year average)
- Total assets ≤ $6.5 million
- Business in operation for at least 2 years
- Good character demonstrated
- Must not have previously participated in 8(a)

**Application Process:**
1. Complete eligibility questionnaire at certifications.sba.gov
2. Register business in SAM.gov
3. Identify primary NAICS codes
4. Submit application via MySBA Certifications portal
5. Provide required documentation (financial statements, tax returns, personal history)
6. SBA review and site visit (if required)
7. Final determination

**Timeline:** 90 days after complete application submission  
**Cost:** FREE  
**Website:** https://certifications.sba.gov/  
**Key Note:** Qualification as minority-owned (Black, Hispanic, Native American, Asian Pacific American, or Subcontinent Asian American)

---

### **SDVOSB (Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business)**
**Administering Agency:** U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA)

**What it gives you:**
- Sole-source contracts up to $5 million (non-manufacturing) / $8.5 million (manufacturing)
- Set-aside contracts competitive among SDVOSBs
- Subcontracting opportunities on large prime contracts
- Priority consideration before other small business programs

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be a small business per SBA size standards
- At least 51% owned by one or more service-disabled veterans
- Service-disabled veteran(s) must control day-to-day operations and strategic decisions
- Service-connected disability rating from VA
- Veteran must have served on active duty and discharged other than dishonorable

**Application Process:**
1. Register in SAM.gov
2. Obtain UEI number (replaces DUNS)
3. Apply via certifications.sba.gov
4. Provide DD-214, disability rating letter, personal and business documentation
5. SBA review and verification
6. Site visit may be required

**Timeline:** 90 days after complete application  
**Cost:** FREE  
**Website:** https://certifications.sba.gov/  
**CRITICAL:** As of January 1, 2024, SBA certification is REQUIRED (self-certification no longer accepted)

## 2. CONTRACT SET-ASIDE PROGRAMS

### **Sole-Source Thresholds (Direct Award Without Competition)**

**8(a) Program:**
- Non-Manufacturing: $4.5 million
- Manufacturing: $7 million
- Entity-owned (tribal/ANC): Above thresholds with justification

**SDVOSB Program:**
- Non-Manufacturing: $5 million  
- Manufacturing: $8.5 million
- VA Contracts: $5 million (via VA's own SDVOSB program)

**HUBZone Program:**
- Non-Manufacturing: $4.5 million
- Manufacturing: $7 million

### **Competitive Set-Asides (Competition Limited to Certified Firms)**

**8(a) Competitive Set-Asides:**
- No upper limit
- Competition restricted to 8(a) participants only
- Minimum 2 qualified 8(a) firms must be available

**SDVOSB Set-Asides:**
- No upper limit
- Minimum 2 qualified SDVOSB firms expected
- Used when reasonable expectation of competition exists

**Small Business Set-Asides:**
- Available to all small businesses
- When 2+ small businesses expected to compete
- Contracts over $250,000 have subcontracting limitations

### **Price Evaluation Preferences**

**HUBZone:** 10% price preference in full and open competition
**8(a):** No statutory price preference (but set-aside opportunities provide advantage)
**SDVOSB:** No price preference in full competition

### **Subcontracting Requirements**

**Contracts over $750,000:**
- Prime contractors must submit subcontracting plans
- Small business subcontracting goals:
  - Small Business: 40%+ of subcontracts
  - Small Disadvantaged Business: 5%
  - WOSB: 5%
  - HUBZone: 3%
  - SDVOSB: 3%

**Limitations on Subcontracting:**
- 8(a)/SDVOSB/HUBZone contracts: Must perform at least 50% of work (services)
- Small business set-asides over $250k: 50% limitation applies
