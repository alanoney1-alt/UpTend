## 1. DwellScan Overview & Requirements

### What is DwellScan?

DwellScan is UpTend's signature property documentation service ‚Äî the **"Carfax for Homes."** It creates a comprehensive baseline record of a property's condition, capturing every system, surface, and appliance through photos, 360¬∞ video, and AI analysis. This data powers the **Property Health Score (1-100)**, feeds insurance partnerships, supports warranty claims, and becomes a transferable asset at resale.

**Why this matters:** You're not just taking photos. You're building a permanent record that helps homeowners:
- Track their property's health over time
- File insurance claims faster with pre-loss documentation
- Prove maintenance for warranty claims
- Qualify for insurance premium discounts
- Transfer a complete property report to buyers at resale

**Your role:** You are the data collector. The better your documentation, the more valuable the homeowner's property intelligence becomes.

## 4. Step-by-Step Execution Protocol

### Phase 1: Exterior Walkthrough (10-15 minutes)

**START IN THE UPTEND APP:**

1. Tap **'Begin DwellScan'** ‚Üí Starts GPS tracking and timer
2. Select tier: **Standard** or **Aerial**
3. Confirm property address matches GPS location

---

**EXTERIOR DOCUMENTATION:**

Walk **clockwise** around the property perimeter, documenting:

#### ROOF (from ground)
- ‚úÖ **4 photos:** front, left, right, rear
- Note shingle condition, age, missing/damaged shingles
- Check for moss, algae, rust stains
- Note chimney condition, flashing
- **Tap 'Add to Property Area: Roof' in app**

#### GUTTERS & DOWNSPOUTS
- ‚úÖ **Photo each side** (4 total)
- Check for sagging, detachment, rust, clogs (visible from ground)
- Note downspout extensions, splash blocks
- **Tap 'Add to Property Area: Gutters'**

#### EXTERIOR WALLS
- ‚úÖ **Photo each wall** (4 minimum)
- Document siding/stucco condition, cracks, water stains
- Check trim, fascia, soffit condition
- Note any mold, mildew, efflorescence
- **Tap 'Add to Property Area: Exterior Walls'**

#### WINDOWS & DOORS
- ‚úÖ **Photo front door, garage door**, any damaged windows
- Check seals, caulking, screens
- Note broken glass, cracked frames

#### DRIVEWAY & WALKWAYS
- ‚úÖ **Photo full driveway, front walkway**
- Note cracks, pitting, oil stains, settling
- Document trip hazards

#### LANDSCAPE
- ‚úÖ **Front/back/side yard overview photos** (3-4 total)
- Note trees close to house (root damage risk)
- Dead/dying plants, drainage issues
- Irrigation system visible components

#### POOL (if present)
- ‚úÖ **Photo pool surface, deck, enclosure** (if screened)
- Document equipment pad with pump/filter visible
- Note cracks, tile damage, deck condition
- Check fence/safety barrier

#### HVAC EXTERIOR UNIT
- ‚úÖ **Photo condenser unit** (2 angles)
- Note age (model plate), condition, clearance
- Check for rust, damage, debris buildup
- **Tap 'Scan Appliance'** ‚Üí Point camera at model plate ‚Üí AI extracts brand/model/serial

---

**APP TIP:**
*As you take each photo, the app auto-categorizes it (AI detects "this is a roof photo"). You can override if wrong. Each photo feeds into the Property Health Score algorithm.*

**For each room:**

   - **Windows** ‚Äî condensation, seal failure, damage
4. **Scan ALL appliances** (see Section 7 for appliance scanning protocol)
5. Note any visible issues: mold, pests, water damage, electrical concerns
6. **Mark room complete** in app

## 6. Photo & Video Quality Standards

### Photo Requirements

**Technical Specs:**
- **Resolution:** Minimum 8MP (most modern phones exceed this)
- **Format:** JPEG or HEIC (app auto-converts)
- **File size:** 2-8MB per photo (app auto-compresses if needed)
- **Lighting:** Natural light preferred; use flash/flashlight for dark spaces
- **Focus:** Tap screen to focus before capturing

**Composition Rules:**

**DO:**
- ‚úÖ Hold phone steady (both hands)
- ‚úÖ Capture full context (not zoomed in too close)
- ‚úÖ Ensure good lighting (open blinds, turn on lights)
- ‚úÖ Clean surfaces before photographing (dust, fingerprints)
- ‚úÖ Take multiple angles of important items
- ‚úÖ Include reference objects for scale (tape measure, hand)

**DON'T:**
- ‚ùå Take blurry photos (app will reject these)
- ‚ùå Photo with finger over lens
- ‚ùå Shoot directly into bright windows (creates glare)
- ‚ùå Use digital zoom (degrades quality)
- ‚ùå Rush ‚Äî take time to frame properly

---

### Model Plate Scanning Best Practices

**For AI to successfully extract data, model plates must be:**

1. **Clean** ‚Äî Wipe with microfiber cloth
2. **Well-lit** ‚Äî Use flashlight if needed
3. **In focus** ‚Äî Tap plate on screen to focus
4. **Fully visible** ‚Äî No obstructions, reflections, or glare
5. **Straight-on** ‚Äî Not at an angle

**If AI Fails to Extract:**
- Try cleaning plate again
- Adjust lighting
- Take multiple photos from different angles
- Manually enter brand/model/serial in app (last resort)

**Common Model Plate Locations:**
- **Refrigerator:** Inside fridge on left/right wall, or back exterior panel
- **Dishwasher:** Top edge of door or inside door frame
- **Oven/Range:** Inside oven door, or back panel
- **Washer/Dryer:** Inside door rim, or back panel
- **Water Heater:** Front panel at eye level
- **HVAC Condenser:** Side panel (exterior unit)
- **Garage Door Opener:** Back or side of motor unit

### Step-by-Step Process (By Task Category)

### **Category 1: TV Mounting**

**Step 1: Pre-Mount Assessment (5 min)**
1. Mark "Arrived" in app
2. Assess with customer:
   - TV size and weight
   - Desired mounting location
   - Wall type (drywall, brick, concrete, tile)
   - Existing outlet location (behind TV?)
   - Cable management needs
3. Take "BEFORE" photo (wall, TV location)

**Step 2: Stud Location (5 min)**
1. Use stud finder to locate studs
2. Mark stud centers with pencil
3. **CRITICAL:** TV mounts MUST anchor into studs (drywall anchors alone are NOT sufficient for TVs over 30")
4. If studs not in ideal location, discuss with customer:
   - Off-center mounting?
   - Use articulating arm mount (allows offset)?
   - Install blocking behind drywall (advanced, charge extra)?

**Step 3: Mount Bracket to Wall (15 min)**
1. Hold bracket at desired height (eye level for seated viewing = 42" to center)
2. Level the bracket (use 4ft level)
3. Mark drill holes
4. Pre-drill pilot holes into studs
5. Attach bracket with lag bolts (provided with mount)
6. **Double-check level** before fully tightening

**Step 4: Attach Bracket to TV (10 min)**
1. Lay TV face-down on soft surface (moving blanket, couch)
2. Attach TV-side bracket arms to VESA mounting holes on back of TV
3. Use bolts provided with mount (or verify size needed)
4. Tighten securely (but don't overtighten = can crack TV housing)

**Step 5: Hang TV (10 min)**
1. Lift TV with bracket arms
2. Hook onto wall bracket (this is a 2-person job for TVs 55"+)
3. Secure locking mechanism (if mount has one)
4. Adjust level (most mounts have small adjustments)

**Step 6: Cable Management & Testing (5 min)**
1. Connect HDMI, power, etc.
2. Use cable concealer (if customer purchased) OR tuck cables neatly
3. Turn on TV (verify it works)
4. Give customer remote, show them how to adjust tilt (if applicable)
5. Take "AFTER" photo

**Step 7: Cleanup & Documentation (5 min)**
1. Vacuum/sweep any drywall dust
2. Dispose of packaging (or leave neatly for customer)
3. Get customer signature in app
4. Mark "Job Complete"

**Common Issues:**
- **Issue:** Studs not where customer wants TV
  - **Solution:** Offer articulating mount or slightly off-center placement. Explain drywall anchors alone can't hold the weight safely.
- **Issue:** TV too heavy for one person
  - **Solution:** Ask customer for help OR call backup Pro. Do NOT attempt solo hang of 65"+ TVs.
- **Issue:** Wires visible, customer unhappy
  - **Solution:** Offer in-wall cable concealment (separate service, requires cutting drywall and fishing cables) OR adhesive cable raceways.

### **Category 3: Drywall Repair**

**Hole Size Determines Method:**

**Small Holes (<2"):**
1. Clean edges of hole
2. Apply spackle with putty knife (thin layer)
3. Let dry 30-60 min
4. Sand smooth (220-grit sandpaper)
5. Apply second coat if needed
6. Prime and paint (if customer provided paint)

**Medium Holes (2-6"):**
1. Cut hole into square/rectangle (drywall saw)
2. Cut drywall patch (slightly smaller than hole)
3. Apply mesh tape around edges
4. Insert patch
5. Screw patch into wood backer strips behind drywall (or use California patch method)
6. Apply joint compound (thin coats, 3 coats total with drying/sanding between)
7. Prime and paint

**Large Holes (6"+):**
1. Cut out damaged section (back to nearest studs)
2. Cut new drywall piece to fit
3. Screw to studs
4. Tape seams with mesh/paper tape
5. Apply joint compound (3 coats, feathered edges)
6. Sand smooth
7. Prime and paint

**Pro Tips:**
- **Matching Texture:** If wall has texture (orange peel, knockdown), use spray texture to match. Bring sample card to show customer options.
- **Paint Matching:** Customer must provide paint. If they don't have leftover paint, offer to color-match at Home Depot (upcharge $20 for trip).
- **Set Expectations:** "The patch will look perfect after 3 coats and paint. It won't be invisible today, but after painting it'll blend completely."

### **Category 4: Minor Plumbing (Faucet Replacement)**

**Step 1: Shut Off Water (5 min)**
1. Turn off under-sink shutoff valves (clockwise to close)
2. Turn on faucet to drain remaining water
3. Place bucket under connections (catches residual water)

**Step 2: Remove Old Faucet (15 min)**
1. Disconnect supply lines (use basin wrench for hard-to-reach nuts)
2. Loosen mounting nuts under sink
3. Lift old faucet out
4. Clean sink surface (remove old putty, grime)

**Step 3: Install New Faucet (20 min)**
1. Read manufacturer instructions (every faucet is slightly different)
2. Assemble faucet parts (some require pre-assembly before installing)
3. Insert faucet through sink holes
4. From underneath, attach mounting hardware (gasket, washers, nuts)
5. Tighten mounting nuts (hand-tight, then 1/4 turn with wrench)
6. Connect supply lines (hand-tight, then 1/4 turn with wrench)
7. **Do NOT overtighten** (cracks plastic fittings)

**Step 4: Test for Leaks (10 min)**
1. Turn water supply back on (slowly)
2. Check for leaks at connections
3. Turn faucet on (both hot and cold)
4. Check under sink for drips
5. If leaking, tighten slightly OR check if washer seated correctly

**Step 5: Cleanup & Documentation (5 min)**
1. Wipe down sink, faucet
2. Dispose of old faucet (or leave for customer)
3. Take "AFTER" photo
4. Get customer signature

**Common Issues:**
- **Issue:** Supply lines too short for new faucet
  - **Solution:** Buy new supply lines at hardware store (braided stainless, 12-20"). Charge customer cost + $10.
- **Issue:** Corroded shutoff valves won't turn
  - **Solution:** Do NOT force (can break valve = major leak). Recommend customer call licensed plumber to replace valves first, then reschedule faucet install.
- **Issue:** Leak persists after tightening
  - **Solution:** Disassemble, inspect washer/gasket (may be damaged or missing). Replace if needed.

### **Category 5: Minor Electrical (Outlet/Switch Replacement)**

**üö® SAFETY FIRST: Turn off circuit breaker, test with voltage tester**

**Step 1: Turn Off Power (5 min)**
1. Identify correct circuit breaker (flip breaker off)
2. Test outlet/switch with voltage tester (non-contact tester)
3. **VERIFY NO POWER** (if tester lights up, you have the wrong breaker)
4. Place tape over breaker (prevents accidental re-activation)

**Step 2: Remove Old Outlet/Switch (5 min)**
1. Remove cover plate (screws)
2. Unscrew outlet/switch from box (top and bottom screws)
3. Pull out gently (wires attached)
4. Take photo of wiring (so you know how to reconnect)
5. Loosen terminal screws, disconnect wires

**Step 3: Install New Outlet/Switch (10 min)**
1. Match wire colors:
   - **Black** = hot (goes to brass/gold screw)
   - **White** = neutral (goes to silver screw)
   - **Green/Bare Copper** = ground (goes to green screw)
2. Bend wire ends into hook shape (clockwise, so tightening screw closes the loop)
3. Attach wires to new outlet/switch (match your photo)
4. Tighten terminal screws (snug, not over-tight)
5. Push outlet/switch back into box (tuck wires neatly)
6. Screw outlet/switch to box
7. Attach cover plate

**Step 4: Test (5 min)**
1. Remove tape from breaker
2. Turn breaker back on
3. Test outlet with tester (should show power)
4. Plug in lamp or phone charger (verify it works)

**Step 5: Documentation (2 min)**
1. Take "AFTER" photo
2. Get customer signature

**Common Issues:**
- **Issue:** Three wires (2 blacks + white + ground)
  - **Solution:** This is a switch loop or 3-way switch. Take clear photo, consult wiring diagram, or decline job if unsure (better safe than sorry).
- **Issue:** Aluminum wiring (silver-colored, not copper)
  - **Solution:** STOP. Aluminum wiring requires special handling and anti-oxidant paste. Recommend licensed electrician.
- **Issue:** Outlet still doesn't work after replacement
  - **Solution:** Issue may be upstream (tripped GFCI, bad wire connection in box, breaker issue). Troubleshoot or recommend electrician.
