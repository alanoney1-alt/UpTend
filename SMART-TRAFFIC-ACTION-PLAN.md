## 2. Product MVP — What Exactly to Build

### 2.1 The Target Product Category

Alan's product should target the **"Traffic Video Analytics / AI Decision Support"** category. This is the sweet spot because:

- FDOT is explicitly asking for it (as seen in the Miami RFP)
- The competition is thin (NoTraffic just got FDOT approval in March 2024; Derq is still piloting)
- It can be built primarily in software (lower hardware costs)
- It integrates with existing FDOT infrastructure (cameras, SunGuide)
- It has clear, measurable value propositions (faster incident detection, better signal timing)

### 2.2 Minimum Viable Product for FDOT Credibility

The MVP doesn't need to be production-ready for a statewide deployment. It needs to be **credible enough that a prime contractor will include it in their proposal** and **demonstrable enough for FDOT APL review**.

#### Core Features (Must Have for MVP):

**1. Real-Time Video Analytics Engine**
- Object detection: vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists, incidents (stopped vehicles, debris, wrong-way drivers)
- Classification: vehicle type (car, truck, bus, motorcycle)
- Counting: volume, speed, occupancy at detection zones
- Built on: **YOLOv8/v9** (open source, proven, fast) running on edge compute or cloud
- Processing: minimum 15 fps on standard CCTV feeds (720p/1080p)

**2. Incident Detection Module**
- Automatic detection of: stopped vehicles, slow traffic, wrong-way vehicles, debris, pedestrians on freeway
- Alert generation with confidence scoring
- Time to detect: <30 seconds from incident occurrence
- False positive rate: <5% (this is the hard part)
- Integration: API that pushes alerts to SunGuide or standard ATMS

**3. Traffic Analytics Dashboard**
- Real-time traffic flow visualization (speed, volume, occupancy by segment)
- Historical trend analysis (hourly, daily, weekly, seasonal patterns)
- Anomaly detection (unusual congestion, demand spikes)
- Performance metrics (travel time reliability, incident clearance times)
- Web-based (accessible from any TMC workstation)

**4. Predictive Analytics Module**
- Short-term traffic prediction (15-minute, 30-minute, 1-hour)
- Congestion prediction based on recurring patterns + real-time data
- Special event impact estimation
- Built on: LSTM or Transformer models trained on FDOT detector data (freely available)

**5. API Integration Layer**
- RESTful API for SunGuide integration
- NTCIP compliance for traffic controller communication
- Standard data formats (TMDD, NTCIP)
- MQTT/WebSocket for real-time event streaming

#### Nice-to-Have (Phase 2):

- Adaptive signal timing recommendations
- Connected vehicle (V2X) data integration
- Multi-modal detection (transit, freight)
- Digital twin visualization
- Automated report generation

## 3. FDOT APL Approval Process — Step by Step

### 3.1 Overview

The FDOT **Approved Product List (APL)** is maintained by the **Traffic Engineering Research Laboratory (TERL)** in Tallahassee. Any traffic control signal, device, or system used on Florida state roads must be on the APL (or have a special permit).

**Key contacts:**
- Derek Vollmer: Derek.Vollmer@dot.state.fl.us
- Matthew DeWitt: Matthew.DeWitt@dot.state.fl.us

**Important nuance:** The APL primarily covers **hardware devices** (signals, controllers, detectors, signs). Software-only platforms may not require traditional APL listing but may need **developmental specification approval** or inclusion through a prime contractor's proposal. This distinction matters — Alan should clarify with TERL early.

### 3.2 The Three-Step Process

#### Step 1: Initial Application (Timeline: ~14 days)

**What to do:**
1. Go to the **PATH (Product Application Tracking and History)** system: https://path.fdot.gov/Specifications
2. Create a vendor account
3. Complete the **Request for Product Consideration (RFPC)** application
4. Submit product documentation including:
   - Product description and specifications
   - Intended use case on Florida roadways
   - Technical documentation
   - Which APL specification category the product falls under

**FDOT responds within 14 calendar days** with one of:
- Product requires APL listing → proceed to Step 2
- Product doesn't fall under APL requirements → different path
- Need more information

**Cost:** Free to apply

#### Step 2: Quality System Evaluation (Timeline: ~30 days)

**What to do:**
1. Submit documentation demonstrating your Quality Management System (QMS)
2. QMS must be based on **ISO 9001 standard** (you don't necessarily need full ISO 9001 certification, but your quality system must align with its principles)
3. Documentation includes:
   - Quality manual
   - Process documentation
   - Inspection and testing procedures
   - Corrective action procedures
   - Document control procedures
   - Training records
   - Supplier quality requirements (if applicable)

**FDOT evaluates within 30 calendar days.**

**Cost:** ISO 9001 certification (if pursuing full cert): $5,000-$15,000. However, FDOT may accept a QMS evaluation without full third-party certification — confirm with TERL.

**Key reference:** [PCH-05: APL Quality Management System Evaluation Process](https://fdotwww.blob.core.windows.net/sitefinity/docs/default-source/traffic/traf_sys/procedural-docs-2022/pch-05-apl-quality-management-system-evaluation-process.pdf)

#### Step 3: Product Evaluation (Timeline: ~60 days total)

**Sub-step 3A: Documentation Review (~14 days)**
1. Submit completed **product compliance matrices** (available at fdot.gov/traffic/traf-sys/product-specifications.shtm)
2. These matrices map your product's features against FDOT's specification requirements
3. FDOT reviews documentation within 14 days

**Sub-step 3B: Product Sample Testing (~45 days)**
1. After documentation approval, submit a **representative sample** of your product to TERL
2. TERL performs functional testing, environmental testing (if hardware), and interoperability testing
3. For hardware: temperature cycling, vibration, humidity, EMI testing
4. For software/AI systems: functional verification, integration testing with SunGuide
5. TERL evaluates within 45 calendar days

**Cost:** Sample submission is free. However, you provide the sample unit (which you may or may not get back). Budget $500-$3,000 for a sample unit.

#### Total APL Timeline: 3-4 months (realistic: 5-6 months with back-and-forth)

### 3.3 The Developmental Process (PCH-09) — Alternative Path

If your product doesn't fit an existing APL specification category (which is likely for a new AI platform), FDOT has a **Developmental Process** under PCH-09:

1. Product is approved on a **developmental/experimental basis**
2. Deployed in a limited, monitored setting
3. Performance data collected over a defined period
4. If successful, can lead to full APL listing or new specification creation

**This is likely Alan's best path** — it's faster, requires less upfront certification, and FDOT is actively looking for innovative technologies to evaluate.

### 3.4 What NoTraffic Did to Get Approved

NoTraffic received FDOT approval in **March 2024** to operate statewide. Their path:

1. **Started with pilot deployments** in specific Florida locations (including Weston and Manatee County)
2. **Demonstrated results** — measurable improvements in traffic flow and safety
3. **Obtained FDOT approval** — likely through the Developmental Process + district-level authorization
4. Their system includes both **hardware (edge sensor units mounted at intersections)** and **software (cloud-based AI platform)**
5. **Key differentiator:** They provide an end-to-end system — sensor + AI + optimization — not just software

**Lesson for Alan:** Start with pilot deployments through a district willing to test new technology. District 5 (Orlando) has been active in AI/innovation (they just deployed Flow Labs' platform in October 2025). Build your FDOT track record through pilots.

### 3.5 What Derq Did

Derq (Detroit/Dubai, MIT spin-off) has been working with FDOT through:
1. **Partnered with VHB** (a large engineering firm) for Florida deployments
2. **Osceola County pilot** — AI video analytics for near-miss detection at intersections

## 4. Certifications Needed — Exact Steps

### 4.1 SDVOSB Certification (SBA VetCert)

**URL:** https://veterans.certify.sba.gov/

**What it is:** Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business certification through SBA's VetCert program. As of January 2024, ALL SDVOSB firms must be certified through SBA (self-certification is no longer valid).

**Requirements:**
- Business must be at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more service-disabled veterans
- The veteran must have a service-connected disability rating from the VA
- Business must be "small" under SBA size standards for its NAICS code
- Relevant NAICS codes for traffic AI:
  - **541511** — Custom Computer Programming Services (size standard: $34M)
  - **541512** — Computer Systems Design Services (size standard: $34M)
  - **541715** — R&D in Physical, Engineering, and Life Sciences (size standard: 1,000 employees)
  - **334290** — Other Communications Equipment Manufacturing (size standard: 1,500 employees)
- Veteran must manage day-to-day operations
- Veteran must hold highest officer position

**Documents needed:**
- DD-214 (discharge papers)
- VA disability rating letter
- Business formation documents (Articles of Incorporation/Organization)
- Operating Agreement or Bylaws
- Federal tax returns (3 years if available, or since inception)
- Business bank statements
- Resume of veteran owner(s)
- SAM.gov registration (must be active — see 4.3 below)

**Timeline:** 90-120 days (SBA processing time)

**Cost:** FREE — no application fee

**Is Alan already certified?** Unknown — needs to check. If Alan is a service-disabled veteran, this should be **top priority**. If not, this entire strategy needs to be reframed around DBE or other small business certifications.

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** If Alan is NOT a service-disabled veteran, the SDVOSB path is not available. In that case, focus on:
- DBE certification (see 4.2)
- SBA 8(a) program (if socially/economically disadvantaged)
- Florida Small Business Enterprise (SBE) certification
- MBE (Minority Business Enterprise) if applicable

### 4.2 DBE Certification with FDOT

**URL:** https://www.fdot.gov/equalopportunity/dbe.shtm
**Phone:** 850-414-4750

**What it is:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise certification required for participation in federally-funded transportation projects.

**Requirements:**
- Business must be at least 51% owned by socially and economically disadvantaged individuals
- Owner(s) must have personal net worth under $1.32 million (excluding primary residence and business ownership)
- Business must be small (meet SBA size standards)
- Owner(s) must control daily operations
- Business must be a for-profit enterprise
- Socially disadvantaged groups include: Black Americans, Hispanic Americans, Native Americans, Asian-Pacific Americans, Subcontinent Asian Americans, and women. Others can apply with documentation of social disadvantage.

**Documents needed:**
- Completed Uniform Certification Application (UCA)
- Personal financial statements
- Business and personal tax returns (3 years)
- Business formation documents
- Bank statements
- Resumes of owners
- List of equipment owned/leased
- Proof of citizenship or permanent residency

**Timeline:** 60-90 days

**Cost:** FREE

**Florida-specific:** FDOT uses the **Florida Unified Certification Program (FL UCP)**. Certification from FDOT is recognized by all Florida DOT-assisted agencies. Apply through FDOT's Equal Opportunity Office.

### 4.3 SAM.gov Registration

**URL:** https://sam.gov/

**What it is:** System for Award Management — required for any entity doing business with the federal government.

**Requirements:**
- Must have an active **UEI (Unique Entity Identifier)** — obtained free through SAM.gov
- Must have an active EIN (Employer Identification Number) from IRS
- Must have a DUNS number (now replaced by UEI)

**Process:**
1. Go to SAM.gov → Create account
2. Get UEI (takes a few days)
3. Complete entity registration (business info, NAICS codes, financial info)
4. Registration is reviewed and activated

**Timeline:** 7-10 business days (can take up to 30)

**Cost:** FREE (beware of scam websites charging fees)

**Must be done BEFORE applying for SDVOSB certification.**

**NAICS codes to register:**
- 541511 — Custom Computer Programming

## 8. Risk Assessment

### 8.1 Risk Matrix

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Alan is not a service-disabled veteran** | Unknown | HIGH — kills SDVOSB strategy | Pivot to DBE, SBE, or 8(a) certification instead |
| **Can't achieve <5% false positive rate** | Medium | HIGH — product not credible | Use ensemble models, human-in-the-loop validation, start with easier use cases |
| **No prime contractor interest** | Low | HIGH — no path to contracts | Attend more events, refine pitch, offer free pilot, consider direct-to-municipality path |
| **FDOT APL process takes 12+ months** | Medium | Medium — delays but doesn't kill | Pursue Developmental Process, work through prime contractor who uses you without APL |
| **Competitor (NoTraffic, Flow Labs) locks up market** | Low-Medium | Medium | Differentiate on price, SDVOSB value, customization, local presence |
| **SunGuide integration proves difficult** | Medium | Medium | Build standalone first, integrate later; SunGuide has documented APIs |
| **Cash flow gap between work and payment** | High | Medium | Keep UpTend revenue flowing, build 90-day cash reserve, negotiate favorable payment terms |
| **Technology doesn't work in Florida conditions** | Low | High | Test early with Florida cameras (rain, sun glare, night); Florida conditions are challenging |
| **Key person risk (only Alan)** | High | High | Document everything, build relationships with multiple primes, have contractor backup plan |

### 8.2 Biggest Unknowns

1. **Is Alan a service-disabled veteran?** This determines the entire certification strategy. The SDVOSB advantage is enormous in government contracting.

2. **What's the realistic incident detection accuracy?** The difference between 90% and 98% accuracy is the difference between a credible product and a toy. This can only be answered by building and testing.

3. **Will prime contractors actually partner with an unknown startup?** The answer is usually yes IF you have (a) a working demo and (b) useful certification. Without both, it's a hard sell.

4. **How competitive is the Florida AI traffic market getting?** NoTraffic (2024), Derq (piloting), Flow Labs (2025) — the window isn't closing yet, but it's getting more crowded. First-mover advantage matters.

5. **What's Alan's technical capability?** Can he build the ML models himself, or does he need to hire an ML engineer? This dramatically affects timeline and cost.

### 8.3 How to Mitigate Each Risk

**Veteran status uncertainty:**
- Determine this IMMEDIATELY — it changes the entire strategy
- If YES → fast-track SDVOSB application (it's the #1 competitive advantage)
- If NO → pursue DBE (if eligible), SBE, or SBA 8(a) instead

**Technical accuracy:**
- Start with the easiest detection tasks (vehicle counting, speed) — high accuracy achievable
- Add harder tasks incrementally (incident detection, wrong-way vehicles)
- Use existing pre-trained models as baseline, fine-tune on Florida data
- Partner with a university (UCF has a strong transportation research center) for validation

**Prime contractor partnerships:**
- Don't rely on one partner — pursue 3-5 simultaneously
- Offer something for free (pilot, demo, technical section for their next proposal)
- Attend EVERY FDOT industry day and networking event
- Join ITS Florida and Florida Section ITE

**Cash flow:**
- Maintain UpTend/other revenue during ramp-up
- Negotiate 15-day payment terms in subcontract (vs standard 30-45)
- Build 90-day operating cash reserve before accepting first subcontract
- Consider SBA microloans or veteran business loans as bridge financing

### 8.4 Kill Criteria — When to Walk Away

Walk away if after 12 months:
- [ ] Zero prime contractor partnerships despite 10+ meetings → market doesn't want what you're building
- [ ] Product can't achieve <10% false positive rate on basic incident detection → technical approach isn't viable
- [ ] SDVOSB/DBE certification denied and no alternative certification path → competitive advantage doesn't exist
- [ ] Total spend exceeds $40K with zero contract pipeline → investment isn't paying off
- [ ] FDOT changes direction away from AI/ML requirements → market disappeared

**Partial pivot indicators** (don't kill, but adjust):
- Prime contractors want different features than you built → pivot product
- APL process is taking too long → focus on non-APL paths (analytics/consulting, not hardware)
- Orlando market saturated → try other districts or states
- Subcontractor margins too thin → consider becoming a direct vendor to municipalities

### 8.5 The Upside Case

If everything works:
- **Year 1:** First subcontract, $100K-$300K revenue
- **Year 2:** 2-3 subcontracts across FDOT districts, $500K-$1M revenue
- **Year 3:** Established FDOT vendor, APL-listed product, $1M-$2M revenue
- **Year 5:** Multi-state expansion (Georgia, Texas, California all have similar programs), $3M-$5M revenue

The smart traffic AI market is projected to be **$15B+ globally by 2030**. Florida alone spends hundreds of millions annually on ITS. Even a tiny slice is a significant business.
