## 1. Federal Grant Programs

### ⚠️ CRITICAL CONTEXT: Current Administration Changes (as of March 2026)

The Trump administration (took office Jan 2025) has renamed, restructured, and in some cases defunded IIJA-era programs. The RAISE program has been **renamed back to BUILD**. The SMART Grants program has been **defunded** with unobligated balances reallocated. Keep this context in mind — programs may be further modified.

### 1A. SMART Grants (Strengthening Mobility and Revolutionizing Transportation) — **EFFECTIVELY DEAD**

- **Official URL:** https://www.transportation.gov/grants/SMART
- **Status:** Congress passed H.R.7148 (Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026) which **reallocated $204,912,000 in unobligated SMART balances**. **No new notices of funding will be issued.**
- **Original funding:** $100M/year, FY2022-2026 ($500M total authorized under IIJA)
- **Who applied:** Public sector agencies (cities, counties, states, tribal governments, transit agencies)
- **SDVOSB relevance:** N/A — program is dead. However, existing Stage 2 awards may still need tech vendors as subcontractors.
- **Source:** https://www.transportation.gov/grants/SMART

**Key takeaway:** Don't pursue this. But look for existing SMART grant recipients in Florida who may need vendors.

### 1D. Carbon Reduction Program (CRP) — **ACTIVE, FORMULA FUNDING**

- **Official URL:** https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/infrastructure-investment-and-jobs-act/crp_fact_sheet.cfm
- **Program page:** https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/environment/crp/
- **Total 5-year funding (FY2022-2026):** **$6.4 billion** nationwide
- **Annual nationwide:** ~$1.3 billion/year
- **Florida's share:** ~2.56% of each state's total FHWA apportionment goes to CRP. Florida receives one of the largest state apportionments (~$3.2B total/year), so Florida's CRP allocation is approximately **$80-85 million/year** (unverified exact figure — calculated from 2.56% of FL's ~$3.3B apportionment)
- **How it works:** Formula funding to states (not competitive grants). FDOT decides how to spend it.
  - 65% must go to urbanized areas (population-based suballocation to MPOs)
  - 35% can go to any area of the state
- **Eligible uses for traffic signal tech:**
  - Traffic signal optimization and coordination
  - Vehicle-to-infrastructure communications equipment
  - Intelligent transportation systems
  - Projects that reduce idling and improve traffic flow
- **Who applies:** States (FDOT) receive funds automatically. Local governments/MPOs work with FDOT to program projects.
- **How a vendor gets involved:** Work with FDOT or MPOs (MetroPlan Orlando) to get your technology included in CRP-funded projects. FDOT decides which projects to fund.
- **SDVOSB advantage:** Federal procurement rules apply to federally-funded projects — DBE goals may be set.
- **Source:** https://www.railstotrails.org/policy/funding/crp/
- **Source:** https://highways.dot.gov/newsroom/president-biden-usdot-announce-new-guidance-and-64-billion-help-states-reduce-carbon

**Key takeaway:** Steady, ongoing money. The pitch: "Our AI traffic optimization reduces vehicle idling by X%, directly reducing carbon emissions — making it an ideal CRP-funded project."

## 4. SDVOSB/DBE Advantage — Specifics

### 4A. Federal SDVOSB Sole-Source Thresholds

- **FAR Reference:** FAR § 19.1406 (https://www.acquisition.gov/far/19.1406)
- **Sole-source thresholds:**
  - **Manufacturing:** Up to **$7 million**
  - **All other (services, etc.):** Up to **$4 million**
- **Requirements for sole-source SDVOSB award:**
  1. Contracting officer does NOT expect two or more SDVOSBs will submit offers
  2. Price does not exceed $7M/$4M threshold
  3. The SDVOSB is a responsible contractor
  4. Award can be made at a fair and reasonable price
  5. **A determination that ONLY one SDVOSB can meet the requirement is NOT required** (Source: https://bvti.com/sdvosb-contracting/)
- **Set-aside rules (FAR § 19.1405):** https://www.acquisition.gov/far/19.1405
  - Contracting officers MAY set aside acquisitions above the micro-purchase threshold ($10,000) for SDVOSB competition
  - Must consider SDVOSB set-asides BEFORE SDVOSB sole-source or general small business set-asides
- **Federal SDVOSB goal:** At least **5% of all federal contracting dollars** set aside for SDVOSBs (Source: https://veterans.certify.sba.gov/)
- **Source:** https://smallgovcon.com/statutes-and-regulations/back-to-basics-small-business-sole-source-awards/

### 4B. SBA SDVOSB Certification Process

- **Application Portal:** https://veterans.certify.sba.gov/ (old) → **https://certifications.sba.gov/** (new MySBA Certifications portal)
- **Processing time:** ~2 weeks once documentation is complete (Source: https://ivmf.syracuse.edu/2025/04/02/how-to-get-your-business-vosb-or-sdvosb-certified/)
- **Eligibility Requirements (13 CFR Part 128):** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-13/chapter-I/part-128/subpart-B
  1. Must be a **small business** under SBA size standards for your NAICS code
  2. At least **51% owned and controlled** by one or more service-disabled veterans
  3. Service-disabled veteran(s) must **reside in the United States**
  4. Must have a VA disability rating (service-connected)
- **Renewal:** Launched November 1, 2025 in MySBA Certifications (Source: https://certifications.sba.gov/)
- **Key change:** SDVOSB verification transferred from VA to **SBA** (Veteran Small Business Certification Act of 2022)
- **Source:** https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/veteran-contracting-assistance-programs

### 4C. SBA 8(a) Business Development Program

- **What it is:** 9-year business development program for disadvantaged small businesses
- **Benefits:**
  - Sole-source contracts up to **$4.5M** (services) / **$7M** (manufacturing)
  - Mentorship, training, technical assistance
  - Access to set-aside and sole-source contracts across ALL federal agencies
  - Joint ventures with large firms while maintaining small business status
- **Eligibility (key requirements):**
  - Small business per SBA size standards
  - At least 51% owned by U.S. citizens who are socially AND economically disadvantaged
  - Service-disabled veterans are **presumed** to be socially disadvantaged
  - Personal net worth must be less than $850,000 (excluding primary residence and business)
  - Must demonstrate potential for success (2+ years in business preferred)
- **Application:** Through https://certifications.sba.gov/
- **Duration:** 9 years (4-year developmental stage + 5-year transitional stage)
- **Can you hold SDVOSB + 8(a)?** YES — they stack. An SDVOSB can also be 8(a) certified.
- **Source:** https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/8a-business-development-program

### 4D. FDOT DBE (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise) Program

- **Official URL:** https://www.fdot.gov/equalopportunity/dbe.shtm
- **FHWA Florida page:** https://highways.dot.gov/field-offices/florida/florida-disadvantaged-business-enterprise-dbe

- **How to certify:** Apply through FDOT's Business Growth and Engagement Office
- **Why it matters:** Being DBE-certified in addition to SDVOSB gives you access to state-funded transportation projects with DBE participation goals

  - **250 intersections:** FDOT installing IMC (Integrated Corridor Management) hardware and firmware across Orange, Osceola, Seminole, and Brevard counties
    - Source: https://www.cflsmartroads.com/projects/ATTAIN-CFL.html

### 5C. Program Components

1. **PedSafe** — Pedestrian/bicycle collision avoidance using CV tech at UCF campus, Alafaya Trail (SR 434), McCulloch Road, Colonial Drive (US 50 near Pine Hills)
   - Hardware installations complete; integration continued into 2021
2. **GreenWay / ICM** — Integrated Corridor Management for I-4. Within 6 minutes, analyzes incidents, predicts traffic 30 min ahead, provides alternate routes, manages signal phases
3. **Connected & Automated Vehicles** — Autonomous vehicle shuttle at UCF campus
4. **Data repository** — Regional TSM&O data storage for research

### 5D. Current Status & Expansion

- Original grant was from ~2018-2019 era (ATCMTD)
- Hardware installations largely complete
- Integration and data collection ongoing
- **Phase 2 / Expansion:** FDOT District 5 continues CV deployments through TSM&O program. The 250-intersection IMC expansion IS the continuation/expansion.
- **Who manages it:** FDOT District 5 Traffic Operations / TSM&O office. CFL SmartRoads is the public-facing operations center.

### 5E. How to Plug Into This Infrastructure

1. **Contact FDOT District 5 TSM&O office** — they manage the existing CV infrastructure
2. **Attend CFL SmartRoads events** — https://www.cflsmartroads.com/
3. **Build compatibility with Applied Information RSUs** — they're the deployed hardware
4. **Offer complementary technology:** AI analytics layer on top of existing SPaT/CV data, predictive signal optimization using their existing infrastructure data
5. **Partner with UCF** — they're already involved in research/data analysis
6. **FDOT Change Management Board:** https://www.fdot.gov/traffic/ITS/Projects-Deploy/cmb.shtm — All changes to SunGuide software (statewide) go through this board
